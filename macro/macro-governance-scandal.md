# Governance Scandals and Institutional Crises — SportMind Macro Intelligence

Corruption investigations, doping programmes, match-fixing revelations, and
institutional governance failures create acute negative signals for the affected
sport and its associated tokens. Unlike most macro events, governance scandals
often originate within sport itself — making them sport-specific rather than
cross-sport events.

Load alongside `macro/macro-overview.md` for the full macro framework.

---

## The FIFA corruption crisis — the defining governance case study

The 2015 FIFA corruption scandal (US Department of Justice indictments, Swiss arrests)
is the largest governance crisis in sports history and the reference point for all
governance scandal intelligence.

```
TIMELINE AND IMPACT:
  May 2015: US DOJ indicts 14 FIFA officials; Swiss police arrest 7 officials at FIFA Congress
  July 2015: Sepp Blatter resigns as FIFA President (re-elected 4 days prior)
  December 2015: UEFA President Platini suspended; further indictments
  Feb 2016: Infantino elected FIFA President; reform process begins
  
COMMERCIAL CONSEQUENCES:
  Major sponsors (Visa, Coca-Cola, McDonald's, Adidas, Budweiser): Public statements
  calling for accountability; did not immediately withdraw but made clear continued
  association was conditional on reform
  
  Fox Sports (US World Cup 2018/2022 rights holder): Continued deal despite scandal
  Broadcast rights: Generally maintained — too much contractual/financial commitment
  
  FIFA revenue: Surprisingly resilient — 2018 World Cup Russia generated record revenue
  The scandal's commercial impact was primarily reputational, not immediately financial
  
TOKEN MARKET LESSON:
  FIFA has no Chiliz fan token; but the lesson is clear:
  Governing body scandals do not immediately destroy the sport's commercial value —
  but they create sustained uncertainty that suppresses new investment, new partnerships,
  and digital product development for 2-4 years after the initial crisis.
```

## Governance scandal taxonomy

### Type 1 — Systemic corruption (governing body level)
The most severe. Affects the credibility of the entire sport.

```
EXAMPLES:
  FIFA 2015: Bribery in rights deals and World Cup hosting decisions
  IAAF doping cover-up (2015-2020): Athletics governing body facilitating doping
  ICC match-fixing investigations (various): Specific to cricket
  
SPORT IMPACT:
  Sponsor uncertainty: Brands review associations; some withdraw; others stay
  Host city withdrawal risk: Countries reconsider bidding for major events
  Athlete trust: Performance results questioned; historical records under review
  
TOKEN MARKET IMPACT:
  Immediate: Sharp negative (news breaks)
  Short-term (1-4 weeks): Uncertainty; reduced trading volume
  Medium-term (1-12 months): Suppressed new token launches; institutional hesitancy
  Long-term (12-36 months): Recovery if reform credible; permanent impairment if not
  
RECOVERY SIGNALS:
  Independent investigation completed with credible findings
  Major governance reform implemented (new leadership, structural changes)
  Major sponsor publicly confirming renewed commitment
  Rights deal successfully renewed (proves commercial viability remains)
```

### Type 2 — Doping and performance enhancement
Systematic doping affects sport credibility and individual athlete token value simultaneously.

```
EXAMPLES:
  Russian athletics programme (RUSADA scandal, 2015-2022)
  Lance Armstrong / US Postal cycling team (1999-2012 period, revealed 2012)
  Ben Johnson 1988 Seoul Olympics
  
SPORT IMPACT:
  Historical results: Retrospective disqualification creates record uncertainty
  Future credibility: New results treated with scepticism
  Broadcast value: Some reduction in rights premiums during scandal periods
  
TOKEN MARKET IMPACT:
  Sport-level: Negative for sport tokens generally; suppresses new launches
  Individual level: Athlete token faces near-total value collapse if athlete confirmed doping
    → Armstrong example: any cycling investment in his era now viewed as fraudulent
  
HIGHEST-RISK ATHLETES FOR TOKEN INVESTMENT:
  Outlier performances (particularly in speed/strength/endurance sports)
  Athletes from historically doping-associated programmes
  Athletes with Athlete Biological Passport anomalies
  Athletes recently returned from unexplained long absences
  
AGENT RULE: For any individual athlete token, research doping history and
current anti-doping standing before any position. A positive test is the
most severe single-athlete token risk event in the library.
```

### Type 3 — Match-fixing
Undermines the fundamental product: an uncertain sporting contest.

```
EXAMPLES:
  IPL match-fixing scandal (2013): Players and franchise owners convicted
  Turkish football match-fixing (Fenerbahçe, 2011): Club found guilty; European competition ban
  Italian football Calciopoli (2006): Juventus relegated; multiple clubs penalised
  Cricket spot-fixing (Pakistan players, 2010): Mohammad Amir et al; ICC sanctions
  
SPORT IMPACT:
  Specific competition: Results questioned; prize money suspended
  Affected clubs: Points deductions, competition bans, financial penalties
  
TOKEN MARKET IMPACT:
  Club found guilty: Severe negative; competition ban removes season of token utility
  Sport-level: Moderate negative; trust in results undermined
  Individual athlete token: Banned athlete = token value collapse (see doping above)
  
MOST VULNERABLE SPORTS (historically):
  Cricket: International betting markets create match-fixing incentives
  Football (lower leagues): Less oversight; match-fixing more documented
  Esports: Growing concern; multiple incidents in CS:GO, LoL regional leagues
  Tennis: 2016 investigation revealed betting alerts on multiple matches
  
ESPORTS MATCH-FIXING — SPECIFIC RISK:
  Multiple documented cases in CS:GO (iBUYPOWER scandal, 2014-15)
  Asian regional leagues particularly at risk
  Esports org tokens directly exposed to match-fixing scandal of their team
  Agent rule: For esports org tokens, monitor integrity unit reports
  (ESIC — Esports Integrity Commission) as a standing input
```

### Type 4 — Athlete welfare and safeguarding failures
Institutional failure to protect athletes from abuse, exploitation, or unsafe conditions.

```
EXAMPLES:
  USA Gymnastics / Larry Nassar abuse scandal (2018): Hundreds of athletes; systemic failure
  Various football club academy abuse investigations (UK, 2016+)
  Qatar World Cup construction worker welfare controversy (ongoing 2010-2022)
  
SPORT IMPACT:
  Sponsor review: Brands sensitive to association with welfare failures
  Governing body credibility: Major reputational damage; sponsorship uncertainty
  Athlete recruitment: Parents/athletes may avoid sports with welfare reputations
  
TOKEN MARKET IMPACT:
  Major sponsors withdrawing: Direct financial impact on club/sport token backing
  Governing body crisis: Suppresses new token launches and partnerships
  Qatari World Cup controversy: Chiliz World Cup products less prominently marketed
    in some markets due to fan boycott sentiment — documented reduced engagement
    in specific European markets despite strong global tournament performance
```

## Governance scandal monitoring and response

```
TIER 1 — IMMEDIATE (act within 24h):
  Arrest of governing body officials
  Club owner criminal indictment or arrest
  Positive doping test confirmed for athlete with personal token
  Match-fixing investigation involving specific club with active token
  
TIER 2 — MONITOR (assess within 72h):
  Governing body investigation announced (not yet indictments)
  Whistleblower allegations published by credible outlet
  Sponsor publicly stating "reviewing" association
  Regulatory body audit of competition management
  
TIER 3 — BACKGROUND:
  General governance concerns raised in media without official investigation
  Historical scandal resurfaces without new evidence
  
AGENT RESPONSE:
  Tier 1: Immediate review; consider exit on tokens directly connected to scandal entity
           Apply Category A macro override for affected sport
           Monitor governance body communication every 12h
  
  Tier 2: Hold current positions with defined exit triggers
           Set price alerts for any meaningful decline
           Assess recovery signals: reform announcement, sponsor confidence statements
  
  Tier 3: Note for future assessment; no immediate action
```

## Recovery signals — governance normalisation

```
CREDIBLE RECOVERY INDICATORS:
  Independent investigation completed with published findings
  New leadership with reform mandate installed
  Major sponsor publicly renewing or increasing commitment
  Major rights deal successfully negotiated (proves commercial confidence)
  New governance structure implemented with external oversight
  
FALSE RECOVERY INDICATORS (do not act on alone):
  Governing body statements without structural change
  New leadership from within the same institutional culture
  Sponsor "considering" returning (verbal, not contractual)
  
RECOVERY TIMELINE:
  Individual athlete doping: 2-4 years (ban period + return to form)
  Club match-fixing (found guilty): 1-3 years (ban period + sporting rebuild)
  Governing body systemic corruption: 3-7 years (FIFA needed ~4 years before new investment confidence)
  Athlete welfare crisis: 2-5 years (systemic safeguarding reform takes time to prove)
```

## Data sources for governance monitoring

- **Court records / DOJ press releases:** Criminal proceedings; most authoritative
- **WADA / USADA:** Anti-doping violations and appeals; official database
- **ESIC (Esports Integrity Commission):** Esports integrity reports
- **TIU (Tennis Integrity Unit):** Tennis match-fixing investigations
- **Investigate journalism:** BBC Sport, The Athletic, Der Spiegel sports investigations
- **Governing body official statements:** Fastest primary source for official responses

## Agent Context Summary

- Governance scandals create 2-7 year suppression periods on new token investment,
  even when the sport itself continues commercially — institutional trust takes time to rebuild
- Individual athlete doping is the most immediate single-athlete token risk; research
  any athlete's anti-doping standing before any personal token position
- Esports match-fixing is the most active current governance concern — ESIC reports
  are a standing monitoring input for any esports org token
- Qatar World Cup welfare controversy demonstrated that non-sporting ethical concerns
  can reduce digital fan product engagement in specific geographic markets
- Recovery is only credible when structural reform is implemented, not just announced

# Geopolitical Events — SportMind Macro Intelligence

Wars, sanctions, diplomatic crises, and political instability have direct, documented
consequences for sporting revenue, athlete eligibility, club participation, and fan
token markets. Unlike pandemics (which affect all sports equally), geopolitical events
are typically sport-specific and geography-specific — but their impact on affected
sports can be total.

Load alongside `macro/macro-overview.md` for the full macro framework.

---

## The Russia-Ukraine war — the defining geopolitical case study

The February 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine produced the fastest and most comprehensive
geopolitical intervention in sports history. The response was sport-by-sport and
accelerating:

```
TIMELINE OF SPORTS CONSEQUENCES (Feb-Mar 2022):

Day 3 (Feb 27): FIFA and UEFA suspend Russia from all competitions
  → Russian clubs removed from Champions League, Europa League, Conference League
  → Russian national team banned from World Cup 2022 qualification playoff

Day 5 (Feb 28): Formula 1 cancels Russian Grand Prix (Sochi)
  → Haas F1 team drops Russian driver Nikita Mazepin and sponsor Uralkali

Day 7 (Mar 1): World Athletics bans Russian and Belarusian athletes
Day 8 (Mar 2): International Olympic Committee recommends exclusion from all events
Day 10 (Mar 4): World Boxing bans Russian and Belarusian boxers
Day 14 (Mar 8): ATP/WTA ban Russian and Belarusian players from team events
               (Wimbledon subsequently bans individually — controversial)

ONGOING (2022–present):
  Russian clubs absent from all UEFA competitions
  Russian national team absent from FIFA competitions
  Russian athletes competing as neutrals (no flag) where permitted
  Belarusian athletes under same restrictions
```

### Token market impact

```
RUSSIAN-LINKED TOKEN CONSEQUENCES:

Clubs with Russian ownership/connections:
  Chelsea FC: Roman Abramovich sanctioned; forced sale (March 2022)
  → Chelsea fan token ($CFG on Chiliz) experienced significant volatility
  → Uncertainty about club ownership created holder uncertainty for weeks

Russian-based tokens (if any):
  Any direct Russian crypto products faced exchange delistings and restrictions
  SWIFT banking sanctions affected ability to purchase tokens in Russian market

GENERAL PRINCIPLE:
  When a major club faces:
  (a) ownership sanctions
  (b) competition ban
  (c) forced administrative change
  
  Token holders face: governance uncertainty, utility disruption,
  community fragmentation, and price volatility.
  
  The Chelsea case demonstrated that a club with 100,000+ token holders
  can navigate ownership transition — but the uncertainty window
  (announcement to confirmed new owner) is the highest-risk period.
```

## Geopolitical event taxonomy

### Type 1 — Active conflict (war, civil war)
Direct impact on sport in or near the conflict zone.

```
SPORT IMPACTS:
  Venue cancellation: Races, tournaments, fixtures in affected territory cancelled
  Athlete displacement: Athletes from conflict zone unable to compete or focus
  Broadcast disruption: Local broadcast infrastructure damaged or redirected
  Sponsor withdrawal: Brands associated with affected nation face commercial pressure
  Fan safety: International fans unwilling to travel; local fans potentially unable to attend
  
DOCUMENTED EXAMPLES:
  Ukraine 2022: Ukrainian Superliga suspended; players evacuated; national team still competing
  Israel 2023-24: Israeli clubs relocated European home matches; UEFA accommodations made
  
TOKEN MARKET BEHAVIOUR:
  Clubs in conflict zone: Token prices typically collapse on conflict escalation
  Adjacent clubs (same nation, outside conflict): Moderate negative; uncertainty
  Opposing nation clubs: Mixed; some solidarity rallies; some negative sentiment
  Recovery: Only when conflict resolves or clubs successfully relocate competitions
```

### Type 2 — Sanctions and bans (diplomatic / punitive)
A governing body or government excludes a nation, club, or individual from competition.

```
SPORT IMPACTS:
  Competition exclusion: Entire national programme removed from qualification
  Individual bans: Athletes unable to compete internationally
  Financial sanctions: Sponsorships frozen; broadcast revenue suspended for affected parties
  Recruitment freeze: Clubs from non-sanctioned countries cannot recruit affected nation's players
  
DOCUMENTED EXAMPLES:
  Russia 2022: Comprehensive sport sanctions (see above)
  WADA Russia doping sanctions (2019-2022): Russian athletes as "ROC" neutral
  North Korea: Effectively excluded from most international sport
  
TOKEN MARKET BEHAVIOUR:
  Immediate: Sharp negative for any token associated with sanctioned entity
  Duration: Persists for full duration of sanction
  Recovery signal: Any official announcement of sanction review or lifting
  
AGENT RULE: Any Chiliz token associated with an entity facing sanction risk
should be assessed for: (a) direct sanction impact on club/nation token,
(b) competition eligibility change affecting token utility, (c) holder
geographic distribution (sanctioned market holders may face access issues).
```

### Type 3 — Diplomatic crises (tension without direct conflict)
Elevated tension between nations that affects sport through boycotts, withdrawals, or policy.

```
DOCUMENTED EXAMPLES:
  1980 Moscow Olympics boycott (US-led, 65 nations)
  1984 Los Angeles Olympics boycott (Soviet-led, Eastern bloc)
  China-Australia diplomatic tensions (2020-22): Affected bilateral sporting relationships
  India-Pakistan: Political tensions periodically affect cricket bilateral series
  Qatar diplomatic crisis (2017-21): Qatar-based athletes affected in some competitions
  
TOKEN MARKET BEHAVIOUR:
  Less immediate than active conflict; builds over weeks/months
  Most significant impact on national team tokens rather than club tokens
  India-Pakistan tensions historically spike uncertainty around bilateral cricket series
  
AGENT RULE: India vs Pakistan cricket matches that coincide with elevated
diplomatic tension have amplified uncertainty on both sides. The match itself
remains the highest-engagement event in cricket — but bilateral series
(longer format) are more at risk of cancellation than ICC tournament fixtures
(third-party neutral organisation controls scheduling).
```

### Type 4 — Host nation political instability
Political instability in a country hosting a major sporting event.

```
RISK PROFILE:
  Tournament cancellation: Extreme instability; governing body relocates event
  Security concern: Fan travel advisories; reduced international attendance
  Government withdrawal of support: Funding cuts to event infrastructure
  
DOCUMENTED EXAMPLES:
  South Africa 2010 World Cup: Security concerns in advance; incident-free
  Brazil 2014 World Cup: Protests; infrastructure delays; event held
  Qatar 2022 World Cup: Human rights controversy; reduced some fan travel;
                         event held; token activity was strong during tournament
  
TOKEN MARKET BEHAVIOUR:
  Pre-event: Negative sentiment building if instability is sustained news story
  During event (if held): Normal sporting event signal dominates
  Cancellation/relocation: Significant negative; equivalent to event cancellation
  
KEY INSIGHT: Governing bodies (FIFA, IOC, UEFA) have strong financial incentives
to hold events as planned. Cancellation requires extreme circumstances.
Pre-event instability is often a stronger negative signal than the event itself warrants.
```

## Sport-specific geopolitical vulnerability

```
HIGHEST GEOPOLITICAL EXPOSURE:
  Football: Most global; most geopolitically integrated; most sanctions history
  Olympics (athletics, swimming, etc.): National team format = direct government association
  Chess: Geopolitically entangled (Russia historically dominant; geopolitics affects governance)
  
MODERATE EXPOSURE:
  Cricket: India-Pakistan axis; bilateral series most vulnerable; ICC tournaments protected
  Tennis: Individual sport; ATP/WTA more independent than national federations
  MMA: Fighter nationality issues (Russian fighters' Chiliz tokens after 2022)
  
LOWER EXPOSURE:
  Esports: More global/distributed; less national team structure; harder to sanction
  Formula 1: No national team structure; driver nationality secondary to constructor
  Horse racing: Primarily domestic markets; international exposure limited
```

## Geopolitical monitoring signals

```
TIER 1 — IMMEDIATE ACTION:
  War declaration or major military action involving a sport-hosting nation
  Major governing body (FIFA, IOC, UCI) suspension or ban announcement
  Government sanctions designation of a club owner or governing figure
  Competition host city under military action or evacuation order

TIER 2 — MONITOR AND PREPARE:
  UN Security Council emergency session on a conflict affecting sports nations
  Multiple nations issuing travel advisories for a sport-hosting country
  Governing body extraordinary meeting called for geopolitical reasons
  Sponsor brands publicly reviewing associations with an affected nation

TIER 3 — BACKGROUND AWARENESS:
  Diplomatic tension escalation between nations with significant sports competition
  Bilateral sports series being threatened by political statements
  Parliamentary/congressional debate on sports sanctions

AGENT ACTION:
  Tier 1: Immediate position review for any tokens connected to affected entity
           Apply Category A macro override (30-50% size reduction)
           Monitor governing body statements every 12h
  
  Tier 2: Prepare contingency positions; hold current positions with alert triggers
  
  Tier 3: No action; background monitoring; note in pre-event assessment
```

## Recovery signals — geopolitical normalisation

```
POSITIVE RECOVERY SIGNALS (consider re-entry):
  Ceasefire or peace agreement confirmed
  Sanctions lifted or significantly relaxed (official announcement)
  Governing body announces reinstatement of suspended entity
  First post-ban international competition participation confirmed
  Major sponsor returns to association with previously sanctioned entity

RECOVERY TIMELINE BY TYPE:
  Active conflict resolution: 6–24 months for full sporting reintegration
  Sanctions lifting: 3–12 months depending on scope
  Diplomatic normalisation: 3–6 months for bilateral sports to resume
  Governance scandal: 6–18 months (see macro-governance-scandal.md)

DOCUMENTED RECOVERY:
  South Africa post-apartheid sports reintegration (1991-92): 
    12 months from ban lifting to full Olympic participation
  Kosovo sports recognition (2014-2016):
    2 years from independence recognition to full UEFA/FIFA membership
```

## Data sources for geopolitical monitoring

- **Reuters / AP / BBC World Service:** Breaking geopolitical news
- **UN Security Council:** Official resolutions affecting nations and therefore sport
- **FIFA / UEFA / IOC / World Athletics official statements:** Governing body responses
- **Foreign & Commonwealth Office (UK) / State Department (US):** Travel advisories
- **OFAC (US Treasury sanctions list):** Individual and entity sanctions
- **CoinGecko / CoinMarketCap:** Token price monitoring during geopolitical events

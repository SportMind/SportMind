# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

SportMind is a technical library. Its community exists to improve sports
intelligence for AI agents through calibration records, skill contributions,
and honest feedback. We are committed to making participation in this project
a straightforward, respectful experience for everyone.

---

## What we expect

**Technical honesty.** Submit calibration records from analyses you actually ran
before matches. Do not backfill predictions with knowledge of results. A wrong
prediction submitted honestly is more valuable than a correct one submitted falsely.
The library's accuracy claims depend entirely on the integrity of its records.

**Analytical neutrality.** Skills should be analytically neutral — they should not
favour any team, athlete, club, or outcome. A skill that encodes a systematic bias
toward a particular club degrades the intelligence it provides to every agent that
loads it. Contributions that appear commercially motivated will not be merged.

**Specific, evidenced feedback.** When reporting inaccuracies in a skill, provide
the specific claim, why it is wrong, and what the correct information is with a
source. "This modifier seems wrong" is not actionable. "The dew_factor modifier for
morning matches is incorrect — dew does not accumulate until evening, see [source]"
is actionable.

**Respect for the review process.** Maintainers review contributions against a clear
quality standard, not personal preference. If a PR is declined, the reason will be
technical and specific. Respond to feedback on the merits. Do not submit the same
rejected contribution unchanged.

---

## What is not acceptable

- Submitting calibration records with knowledge of the outcome
- Contributing skills that encode factual inaccuracies and refusing correction
- Claiming contribution credit for work not your own
- Personal attacks on contributors or maintainers
- Harassment of any kind in issues, PRs, or discussions
- Coordinated efforts to influence modifier recalibration by submitting biased records

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all SportMind project spaces — the GitHub repository,
issues, pull requests, and any community spaces created for SportMind discussion.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this standard. Unacceptable behaviour
may result in temporary or permanent removal from the project. Decisions are final.

Report concerns to the maintainers via a private GitHub message or the contact listed
in `SECURITY.md`.

## Annual Tournament Calendar — Peak Windows for Token Intelligence

### January–March: Slow start, qualifiers
- CS2: IEM Katowice (Feb) — first Tier 1.5 event of year
- LoL: LEC/LCS Spring Split begins (January)
- Valorant: VCT kickoff events
- Dota 2: DPC regional leagues begin
- **Token signal:** Low-moderate. Season establishing. Watch for roster confirmations.

### April–June: Spring peaks
- CS2: ESL Pro League Season (May-Jun) — Tier 2, but signals form
- LoL: MSI (May) — Tier 1.5, international; OrgTIS 80 for advancing orgs
- Valorant: VCT Masters (May) — Tier 1.5
- **Token signal:** Moderate-high for advancing orgs. MSI = first major international test.

### July–September: Summer peaks + transfer window
- CS2: IEM Cologne (July) — Tier 1.5, massive audience
- LoL: LEC/LCS Summer Playoffs (August)
- Valorant: VCT Champions (August–September) — Tier 1 annual peak
- **Transfer window:** August–September — highest roster movement period
- **Token signal:** High. Valorant Champions = peak Valorant event. CS2 IEM Cologne = major event.
- **RSI warning:** Transfer window means RSI scores volatile. Recheck weekly.

### October–November: PEAK SEASON (Multi-stack window)
- CS2: CS2 Major (October) — Tier 1, highest OrgTIS in CS2 calendar
- LoL: Worlds (October–November) — Tier 1, highest viewership in all esports
- Dota 2: The International (October) — Tier 1, highest prize pool
- Valorant: post-Champions cool-down; regional finals
- **Token signal:** MAXIMUM. Multi-Tier-1 stack window.
  For orgs competing in CS2 Major + Worlds: apply ×1.35 OrgTIS multiplier.

### December: Season wind-down
- LoL: Worlds final (early November bleeds into December)
- Roster shuffle begins (contracts expire Dec 31 in many games)
- **Token signal:** Declining but narrative-heavy (roster announcement season)

## Patch Impact History — Key Examples for Calibration

### CS2 — Major patch impact examples
- Operations (map rotations): moderate impact, teams with map pool overlap benefit
- Gun meta changes (AK/rifle tuning): high impact for specific playstyle teams
- Typical PRS after major operation: 25–40

### League of Legends — Patch cadence impact
- LoL patches every 2 weeks; Worlds patch is locked ~2 weeks before tournament
- "Worlds patch" determination is a major strategic signal — which champions are strong?
- Teams known as "meta slavers" (play only OP champions) have high PRS exposure
- Teams known as "flex pickers" (adapt quickly) have low PRS exposure
- Typical PRS at Worlds: 15–30 (known patch, teams prepared)
- PRS spike: 50–70 if Riot releases emergency balance patch mid-split

### Valorant — Agent and map pool changes
- New agent releases always create elevated PRS for 1–2 tournaments
- Map pool rotations (every 6 months) shift team advantages significantly
- Typical PRS in regular season: 25–45
- Typical PRS at Champions: 15–25 (meta well-established by then)

### Dota 2 — Major update cycles
- Dota patches are infrequent but sweeping — can change hero viability completely
- Pre-TI patch (released ~1–2 months before) is the most-watched game event in Dota
- Teams that dominated pre-patch may be completely reshuffled post-patch
- Typical PRS post-major Dota update: 60–80 (highest of any game)

## Token Decay Curves — Esports Events

| Event | Day 1 | Day 3 | Day 7 |
|---|---|---|---|
| CS2 Major win | 100% | 82% | 55% |
| LoL Worlds win | 100% | 88% | 65% |
| Dota TI win | 100% | 85% | 60% |
| Valorant Champions win | 100% | 80% | 52% |
| Major group stage exit | 100% | 75% | 50% (sustained negative) |
| Star player transfer announced | 100% | 70% | 38% |
| Roster stand-in confirmed | 100% | 80% | 60% (sustained negative) |

**Key insight:** Negative events (exits, roster problems) sustain longer than
equivalent positive events in esports — similar to MMA. The uncertainty around
future roster stability keeps the negative pressure active.

## RSI Rapid-Check Protocol

Before entering any esports token position, run this rapid RSI check:

```
1. Check: Any contract expiry announced within 90 days? → +35% change probability
2. Check: Last 3 tournament results — is team declining? → +20% per decline
3. Check: Any public player-org dispute on social media? → +25%
4. Check: Head coach change in last 60 days? → +20%
5. Check: Any stand-in player used in last tournament? → +15%

If total probability > 40%: reduce position sizing 30%
If total probability > 60%: wait for roster confirmation before entering
If full roster rebuild confirmed: treat as new org, historical signals invalid
```

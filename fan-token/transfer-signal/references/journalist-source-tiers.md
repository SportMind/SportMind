# Transfer Source Tier Reference

## Tier 1 — 0.90–0.95 weight
Journalists and sources with proven accuracy on major transfers. "Here we go" = near-certain.
- Fabrizio Romano (@FabrizioRomano) — global, highest accuracy
- David Ornstein (@David_Ornstein) — Premier League specialist, The Athletic
- Gianluca Di Marzio (gianlucadimarzio.com) — Serie A specialist
- Florian Plettenberg (@Plettigoal) — Bundesliga specialist, Sky Germany
- Official club announcements — 1.0 weight (confirmed)

## Tier 2 — 0.65–0.80 weight
Established sports media with good but not infallible transfer records.
- Sky Sports News (@SkySportsNews)
- BBC Sport (@BBCSport)
- The Athletic (various journalists)
- Marca (Spanish clubs)
- Gazzetta dello Sport (Serie A)
- L'Équipe (French clubs, Ligue 1)
- AS (Spanish clubs)
- Bild (Bundesliga)

## Tier 3 — 0.40–0.60 weight
Aggregators, regional press, or outlets with mixed track records.
- transfer news aggregator sites
- Regional/local club press
- Football.london, Manchester Evening News (club-specific beat reporters)
- Goal.com, 90min (aggregators)

## Tier 4 — 0.10–0.30 weight
Fan accounts, forums, social media speculation. Useful for early signal, not confirmation.
- Football Twitter/X fan accounts
- Reddit transfer threads (r/soccer, club subreddits)
- Fan blogs

## Confidence language patterns (NLP markers)
High confidence: "here we go", "done deal", "agreement reached", "contract signed", 
                 "medical scheduled", "personal terms agreed", "confirmed"
Medium confidence: "close to agreeing", "advanced talks", "negotiations ongoing",
                   "bid accepted", "offer made"
Low confidence: "interested in", "monitoring", "considering", "could move",
                "rumoured", "linked with", "eyeing"
Denial markers: "not interested", "not in our plans", "happy at club", "committed",
                "denied by club", "no contact"

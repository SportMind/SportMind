# Adaptation Curves by Transfer Type

## Same league, different club
Expected adaptation: 4–8 weeks
Curve: Weeks 1–2 at 80–90% of previous PI. Full capability by week 7.
Accelerated by: existing teammates, same tactical philosophy.

## Domestic promotion (lower to top league)
Expected adaptation: 8–16 weeks
Curve: Months 1–2 at 65–80% translated PI. Full by month 5.
Key challenge: pace, pressing intensity, technical quality all increase sharply.

## Overseas transfer (similar tier, different country)
Expected adaptation: 12–20 weeks
Language barrier: no language = 4 weeks faster on average.
Style delta (high-press to possession): add 8+ weeks.
Curve: Months 1–3 at 60–80%. Full capability months 7–9.
Red flag: Still below 75% PI at 5 months = environment may not suit.

## Development loan (lower division)
Ramp-up: 2–4 weeks (lower intensity = faster comfort).
Week 3–6: Should be establishing in team. If not, manager trust issue.
Warning: Minutes below 50% in first 8 weeks = loan not working.

## Post-serious-injury return (>4 weeks out)
Expected recovery to baseline: 10–15 matches post-return.
Forwards: -20% xG/goal output for first 10 matches.
Midfielders: -10% progressive actions for 6 matches.
Defenders: Physical recovery fast; positioning takes longer.
Flag: Still -15%+ below pre-injury PI after 15 matches = potential long-term impact.

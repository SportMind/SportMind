# Contract Clause Types and Risk Frameworks

## Sell-On Clauses
Selling club receives % of profit/fee when player is re-sold.
Standard ranges: 5–10% low / 15–20% standard / 25–30% high / 50% rare (co-ownership)
Risk: Above 20% meaningfully limits future sale profit.
Recommendation: Cap at 15%; accept 20% for top-tier talent.

## Release Clauses
Fixed fee at which player can leave regardless of club's wishes.
La Liga: Legally mandated. Set at 3–10x annual salary typically.
Premier League: Not legally required; used occasionally.
Risk assessment:
- Clause <150% of current value: Vulnerable to rivals triggering
- 200–250%: Acceptable protection
- >300%: Strong (functionally blocking)

## Buy-Back Clauses
Selling club option to repurchase at fixed fee within a timeframe.
Risk to buying club: Fixed price below expected future value will be triggered.
Recommendation: Avoid entirely if possible; if required, price at 130%+ of current value.

## Wage Structure Flags (Premier League benchmarks)
High earner thresholds by position:
- GK: >£80K/week
- CB: >£100K/week
- FB: >£90K/week
- CM: >£110K/week
- CF/FW: >£150K/week

Risk flags:
- Player earning 2.5x nearest squad member: Dressing room tension risk
- Long-term deal (5+ years) at peak wages: Value deteriorates post-30

# Fan Token Partnership Intelligence — SportMind Layer 3

Commercial intelligence framework for fan token partnerships — from new partnership
announcement signals through active health monitoring to termination events and
post-contractual token trajectory assessment.

Read alongside `fan-token/fan-token-lifecycle/fan-token-lifecycle.md` for the complete
lifecycle framework. This skill focuses specifically on the partnership relationship
between a club/athlete/sport and a fan token platform (primarily Chiliz/Socios).

---

## Partnership intelligence overview

```
THREE TYPES OF PARTNERSHIP EVENTS — ALL ARE SIGNIFICANT SIGNALS:

1. NEW PARTNERSHIP ANNOUNCEMENT
   A sport, club, or athlete previously without a token enters the ecosystem.
   Signal type: Strong positive for CHZ; moderate-to-strong positive for related tokens
   Agent action: Assess launch quality indicators; model initial holder acquisition curve
   
2. PARTNERSHIP RENEWAL OR EXPANSION
   Existing partnership renewed, extended, or expanded to new markets.
   Signal type: Positive confirmation signal; reduces termination risk premium
   Agent action: Update lifetime token utility index; reassess Phase trajectory
   
3. PARTNERSHIP TERMINATION OR LAPSE
   Official partnership ends. Token continues on-chain non-contractually.
   Signal type: Negative for affected token; neutral-to-positive for non-contractual
               token long-term if third-party ecosystem develops
   Agent action: Transition to non-contractual token framework (lifecycle skill)
```

## Part 1 — New partnership announcement intelligence

### Why new partnerships are among the largest fan token signals

A new partnership announcement does three things simultaneously:

```
CHZ-LEVEL IMPACT:
  New partnership = new token minted on Chiliz chain
  = new demand for CHZ to purchase the token
  = validation of Chiliz platform commercial momentum
  = media coverage driving new user awareness
  Net: CHZ positive catalyst; typically +5–15% in 24–48h for major partnerships

SPORT/CLUB TOKEN IMPACT:
  Pre-launch: No token exists; anticipation builds from announcement to launch
  Launch day: Highest single-day volume in the token's life (usually)
  Post-launch: Holder acquisition curve begins

ECOSYSTEM IMPACT:
  Large partnership (Premier League club, UFC fighter, F1 constructor):
    Positive spillover to related tokens in same sport
  New sport category entry (first NBA team, first NASCAR team):
    Positive for CHZ; creates new sport-specific token market
  Market-creating entry (Chiliz entering a new geographic market):
    Largest possible single-partnership impact; creates new buyer pool
```

### New partnership signal categories

```
TIER 1 — MARKET-CREATING PARTNERSHIPS (highest impact):
  First club token in a major sports market with no existing tokens
  Examples: First NFL team token, first IPL franchise token, first J-League club
  CHZ impact: +10–25% (documented range for comparable past events)
  Sport impact: Entire sport segment re-rated; related Tier 2/3 tokens benefit
  
TIER 2 — MAJOR CLUB / ATHLETE ADDITIONS (significant):
  Top-5 clubs in an already-tokenised league add a token
  Examples: A historically untokenised Champions League regular joining
  CHZ impact: +5–12%
  Sport impact: Raises average token quality in segment; positive for peers
  
TIER 3 — STANDARD CLUB ADDITIONS (moderate):
  Mid-table or lower clubs in already-tokenised leagues
  Examples: Newly promoted Bundesliga club adding token
  CHZ impact: +1–5%
  Sport impact: Minor; increases ecosystem breadth

TIER 4 — ATHLETE INDIVIDUAL TOKENS (variable):
  Individual athlete token (not club/team-level)
  Examples: Fighter token, individual player token
  CHZ impact: Minimal for most; exception for global superstars
  Sport impact: Creates athlete-level prediction and sentiment signal

TIER 5 — SPORT GOVERNING BODY / COMPETITION TOKENS:
  Tournament, league, or federation tokens (not club-level)
  Examples: Champions League token, World Cup token, NBA league token
  CHZ impact: High (governing body = entire sport market entry)
  Sport impact: Creates competition-level signal across all participating clubs
```

### Due diligence framework — assessing partnership quality pre-launch

```
PARTNERSHIP QUALITY ASSESSMENT (before a token launches):

INSTITUTIONAL SIGNALS:
  ✅ Strong:  Club CEO or President at announcement; formal press conference
  ✅ Strong:  Multi-year partnership agreement publicly stated
  ✅ Strong:  Chiliz listed as official partner on club website
  ✅ Strong:  Launch utility event confirmed and dated before listing
  ⚠️ Weak:   Social media announcement only (no formal event)
  ⚠️ Weak:   Single-year initial agreement
  ❌ Warning: Club representatives not appearing in any public communication
  ❌ Warning: Launch utility event not confirmed at announcement

MARKET SIGNALS:
  ✅ Strong:  Binance or top-3 CEX listing confirmed at launch
  ✅ Strong:  Geographic alignment (club's actual fanbase market accessible)
  ✅ Strong:  Club fanbase has documented digital engagement (social following)
  ⚠️ Weak:   No CEX listing confirmed — DEX only at launch
  ❌ Warning: Club in active financial distress at time of partnership announcement
  ❌ Warning: Announcement during poor sporting period (relegation zone, etc.)

PARTNERSHIP STRUCTURE SIGNALS:
  ✅ Strong:  Exclusive platform arrangement (Chiliz is sole token platform)
  ✅ Strong:  Revenue sharing component (club has financial incentive to promote)
  ✅ Strong:  Utility roadmap published with specific dates/events
  ⚠️ Weak:   Non-exclusive; club may have multiple digital asset partnerships
  ❌ Warning: No published utility roadmap
  ❌ Warning: Partnership announcement coincides with club seeking emergency funding

COMPOSITE ASSESSMENT:
  5–6 ✅ Strong signals: High-quality launch; hold/accumulate before listing
  3–4 ✅ Strong signals: Standard launch; accumulate on post-launch dip
  1–2 ✅ Strong signals: Below-average quality; monitor only
  Any ❌ Warning signals: Apply significant discount to initial valuation
```

## Part 2 — Partnership health monitoring

### The five health indicators

```
PARTNERSHIP HEALTH SCORE (PHS) — composite of five indicators:

1. UTILITY EVENT FREQUENCY (UEF)
   Rolling 90-day count of official utility events:
   ≥4 events: 1.0 (healthy)
   2–3 events: 0.7 (declining)
   0–1 events: 0.3 (critical)

2. CLUB SOCIAL PROMOTION (CSP)
   Quality of club social account engagement with token:
   Club actively promoting: 1.0
   Socios/Chiliz promoting but not club: 0.5
   No promotion from either: 0.1

3. HOLDER COUNT TREND (HCT)
   Rolling 30-day holder count change:
   Growing >+2%: 1.0
   Flat ±2%: 0.7
   Declining 2–10%: 0.4
   Declining >10%: 0.1

4. TOKEN UTILITY INNOVATION (TUI)
   New utility types introduced in last 6 months:
   New utility introduced: 1.0
   Same utility types as 6 months ago: 0.6
   Reduced utility types: 0.2

5. PARTNERSHIP DURATION SIGNAL (PDS)
   Time to next renewal vs average partnership length:
   >12 months to renewal: 1.0
   6–12 months to renewal (renewal not announced): 0.6
   <6 months to renewal (renewal not announced): 0.3

6. VALIDATOR STATUS INDICATOR (VSI) — applies to validator clubs only
   Club's status as a Chiliz Chain validator node:
   Active validator, stable or growing stake: 1.0
   Active validator, minor stake decline (<10%): 0.8
   Active validator, significant stake decline (>10%): 0.5
   Validator status uncertain / governance participation declining: 0.2
   Validator status ended or being unwound: 0.0
   Not a validator (default for most clubs): NOT SCORED — use 5-indicator PHS

STANDARD PHS (non-validator clubs):
  PHS = Average(UEF, CSP, HCT, TUI, PDS)

VALIDATOR-ADJUSTED PHS (validator clubs e.g. PSG):
  PHS = Average(UEF, CSP, HCT, TUI, PDS, VSI) × 1.10
  The 1.10 multiplier reflects the structural commitment premium —
  a validator club at 0.70 is genuinely more committed than a non-validator
  at 0.70 because exit cost is materially higher.
  Validator PHS floor: VSI = 1.0 prevents PHS falling below 0.50.
  See fan-token/blockchain-validator-intelligence/ for full VSI framework.

PHS ≥ 0.75: Healthy partnership — full Layer 3 applicable
PHS 0.50–0.74: Plateau phase — reduce Layer 3 weight by 30%
PHS 0.25–0.49: Declining partnership — prepare for Phase 5 transition
PHS < 0.25: Terminal decline — begin non-contractual token assessment
```

### Early warning signals of partnership deterioration

```
TIER 1 WARNING SIGNALS (act immediately on token positions):
  Club changes commercial director without announcement of token continuity
  Club enters administration, receivership, or major financial restructuring
  No utility event for 90+ days with no announced upcoming event
  Chiliz removes club from official website partnership listings
  Club's Socios app page becomes inactive or redirects

TIER 2 WARNING SIGNALS (monitor closely):
  Club social accounts stop tagging Socios/Chiliz in posts (60+ days)
  Token holder count declining >5% for 3 consecutive 30-day periods
  Club announces new commercial partnerships that crowd out token visibility
  Chiliz announces a new competing club token in the same market
  Club ownership change without statement on digital partnerships

TIER 3 BACKGROUND MONITORING:
  Reduced frequency of utility events (but still occurring)
  Club performance decline (relegation risk elevates financial pressure)
  Key club partnership champion (e.g., Chief Digital Officer) leaves role
  General reduction in club digital engagement activity

AGENT RESPONSE TO TIER 1 SIGNALS:
  Reduce token position by 40–60%
  Set price alert for -15% from current (additional warning trigger)
  Begin monitoring for termination announcement
  Load non-contractual token assessment framework
  Assess: would this token have prediction market value post-termination?
```

## Part 3 — Partnership termination events

### How terminations typically occur

```
TERMINATION PATTERNS (observed across the Chiliz ecosystem):

PATTERN 1 — ANNOUNCED TERMINATION (rare; most honest):
  Club/Chiliz issues formal statement that partnership is ending.
  Timing: Often at contract expiry (not mid-contract).
  Token impact: Immediate sharp decline (-20 to -40% in 24–48h)
  Recovery: Partial recovery over weeks as market establishes non-contractual value
  
PATTERN 2 — SILENT LAPSE (most common):
  Partnership contract expires; not renewed; no public announcement.
  Utility events simply stop occurring.
  Token continues trading; no formal statement.
  Agent challenge: Distinguishing silent lapse from extended plateau (Phase 4)
  Signal to monitor: 180+ days of zero utility events = effectively non-contractual
  
PATTERN 3 — FORCED TERMINATION (ownership/regulatory events):
  External event forces partnership end:
  Examples: Club owner sanctions (Chelsea/Abramovich 2022), 
            regulatory action (FTX-related tokens),
            club insolvency
  Token impact: Most severe; typically -40 to -70% in 24–48h
  Recovery: Slowest; dependent on resolution of underlying event

PATTERN 4 — SPORT CATEGORY EXIT (platform-level):
  Chiliz/Socios exits an entire sport category.
  All tokens in that category affected simultaneously.
  Unlikely for major sports; possible for experimental categories.
  Token impact: Category-wide decline; all affected tokens simultaneously

TERMINATION CONFIRMATION SIGNALS:
  Chiliz removes club from official platform listings
  Club removes token from official digital channels
  Formal press release (rare but definitive)
  180+ days of zero utility events after active partnership period
  Exchange formally categorises token as "community" vs "official partner"
```

### Termination impact framework

```
TERMINATION IMPACT ASSESSMENT:

IMMEDIATE IMPACT (first 30 days):
  Trading volume: -40 to -70% decline
  Holder count: -10 to -25% (dilettante holders exit)
  Price: -20 to -50% (depends on club profile and pattern type)
  CEX status: Unchanged initially; review period begins

SHORT-TERM (1–6 months):
  Volume stabilises at lower equilibrium
  Holder base consolidates (remaining holders are committed)
  CEX review: Large club tokens likely survive; smaller tokens at delisting risk
  DEX liquidity: Stable or slightly increasing as DeFi-native holders emerge
  Price: Finds new floor; begins correlating more cleanly with sporting performance

MEDIUM-TERM (6–18 months):
  BIFURCATION POINT: Tokens either:
  PATH A — Develop non-contractual ecosystem (third-party use cases, prediction markets)
            → Sustained value; potential for new utility layer
            → Possible re-rating as independent community asset
  PATH B — Gradual abandonment (volume declines to de minimis)
            → CEX delisting likely; DEX-only residual value
            → Effectively dormant on-chain (but still technically tradeable)

PATH A TRIGGERS:
  Third-party developer integrates token into prediction market or GameFi
  Community DAO forms around token governance
  New exchange listing as "community token" (some platforms specialise in this)
  Club re-approaches market for new partnership (potential relaunch catalyst)

PATH B TRIGGERS:
  CEX delisting without replacement
  Core community Discord/Telegram drops below critical mass
  No third-party development activity for 12+ months
  Club loses relevance in its sport (sustained poor performance + relegation)
```

## Part 4 — Partnership case studies

### Important framing note

```
ACCURACY REQUIREMENT:

Fan token partnership statuses change. Before treating any token as
non-contractual in agent reasoning, verify current status against:
  → Chiliz/Socios official platform listings
  → Club official website (partner pages)
  → CoinGecko/CoinMarketCap token description
  → Recent club social media activity referencing the token

The case studies below are categorised as:
  TYPE A — Active partnership with documented predictive utility behaviour
           (token still contractual; insight is about how predictive utility
           emerges WITHIN active partnerships under stress conditions)
  TYPE B — Lapsed or reduced engagement (partnership status uncertain
           or officially reduced; treat as indicative, verify before use)
  TYPE C — Confirmed non-contractual / effectively dormant

This distinction matters. Labelling an active partnership token as
non-contractual would cause an agent to incorrectly deprioritise
Layer 3 signals and misframe the token's commercial position.
```

### Type A — Active partnerships exhibiting predictive utility behaviour

```
CASE STUDY A1: JUVENTUS $JUV — ACTIVE SOCIOS PARTNERSHIP
Status: ACTIVE contractual Socios/Chiliz partnership
        $JUV trades on Binance; one of the highest-liquidity football fan tokens
        Club provides ongoing fan utility: voting, exclusive content, experiences
Exchange: Binance listed; strong CEX presence maintained
        
IMPORTANT CORRECTION: $JUV is an active partnership token, not non-contractual.
Any agent assessment must treat $JUV with full Layer 3 stack applicable.

BEHAVIOURAL INSIGHT (does not imply non-contractual status):
During Juventus's 2022–23 institutional crisis — points deductions, legal
proceedings, ownership uncertainty — $JUV price showed elevated correlation
with institutional (court, ownership) outcomes, not just match results.

This illustrates a documented phenomenon: CRISIS CORRELATION AMPLIFICATION.
Under institutional stress, even active-partnership tokens temporarily shift
from governance-utility-driven pricing toward prediction-market-style pricing.
The market is pricing the SURVIVAL of the underlying institution, not the
quality of utility events.

Key intelligence: When a club faces major institutional stress (ownership
dispute, financial crisis, governance investigation), temporarily increase
the weight of institutional signal inputs and reduce reliance on standard
utility-event signals — even for active-partnership tokens.

LESSON FOR THE LIFECYCLE MODEL:
The predictive utility mode is not exclusive to non-contractual tokens.
It emerges in active-partnership tokens under institutional stress.
This is the earliest visible signal of potential Phase 4 drift.

CASE STUDY A2: AC MILAN $ACM — ACTIVE SOCIOS PARTNERSHIP
Status: ACTIVE contractual Socios/Chiliz partnership
        $ACM trades on Binance; active Italian market engagement
        Club provides ongoing fan utility including voting and exclusive content
Exchange: Binance listed; Italian-market-concentrated holder base

IMPORTANT CORRECTION: $ACM is an active partnership token, not non-contractual.
Any agent assessment must treat $ACM with full Layer 3 stack applicable.

BEHAVIOURAL INSIGHT (does not imply non-contractual status):
Following the Elliott → RedBird Capital ownership transition (2022), there
was a documented period of commercial restructuring during which $ACM utility
event communications were less prominent from the club's own social channels
(as opposed to Socios channels). This is a Phase 4 plateau warning signal
pattern — not a termination.

On-chain holder analysis during this period showed geographic concentration
in Italy and UK consistent with genuine fanbase composition rather than
speculative concentration — indicating a committed holder base that would
likely persist through any future utility lapse.

LESSON FOR THE LIFECYCLE MODEL:
Ownership transitions are one of the highest-risk periods for Phase 4 drift.
New ownership groups reassess all commercial partnerships. Monitor club social
promotion quality (CSP component of PHS) especially closely during the 6–12
months following any ownership change, even for top-tier active tokens.
```

### Type B — Reduced engagement / uncertain status

```
CASE STUDY B1: FAZE CLAN $FAZE (ESPORTS)
Status: UNCERTAIN — FaZe Clan's corporate restructuring and SEC-related
        complications created a period where official promotion of $FAZE
        became significantly reduced. Partnership status is not definitively
        confirmed as terminated in public documentation as of the last update
        to this file. VERIFY CURRENT STATUS before any agent use.
Exchange: Was listed on multiple exchanges; volumes declined significantly
        during the corporate restructuring period

Context: FaZe Clan's challenges — SPAC listing complications, regulatory
involvement of FaZe members, organisational changes — meant $FAZE official
promotion declined markedly. Whether this constitutes a formal termination
or an extended Phase 4 plateau is not confirmed publicly.

BEHAVIOURAL INSIGHT:
On-chain holder data during the reduced-promotion period showed FaZe's
genuine global esports fanbase remained present. Token price showed elevated
correlation with FaZe Clan's esports competitive results compared to the
active-promotion period — consistent with the crisis correlation amplification
pattern observed in Case Study A1.

KEY COMPLEXITY FOR ESPORTS TOKENS:
Esports org token status is harder to verify than football club tokens because:
  → No single governing body maintaining official token registry
  → Org may continue token while officially distancing from it
  → Publisher IP questions create ambiguity about what the token represents
  → Roster changes can affect the token's identity more than ownership changes
  → SEC regulatory history (in $FAZE's case) creates additional uncertainty

LESSON: Always verify current status for esports tokens; treat any token
with reduced official promotion as Phase 4 until status confirmed.
Esports tokens require more frequent status verification than club tokens.

CASE STUDY B2: INDIAN SUPER LEAGUE (ISL) CLUB TOKENS
Status: REDUCED/UNCERTAIN — Several ISL tokens launched during Socios's
        2021-22 Indian expansion had minimal utility engagement post-launch.
        Whether these constitute formal terminations or extended Phase 4
        plateaus with potential for renewal is not uniformly documented.
        VERIFY CURRENT STATUS for each token individually before agent use.
Exchange: Limited CEX presence at launch; primarily smaller exchanges or DEX

Context: Socios's broader Indian strategy faced friction — regulatory
uncertainty, club engagement challenges, market education requirements.
Several ISL tokens had minimal utility activity within months of launch,
consistent with Phase 4 plateau behaviour rather than full Phase 5/6.

Key insight: These tokens demonstrate the importance of launch quality
assessment. Tokens launched without institutional depth (no CEX listing,
no sustained utility roadmap, regulatory uncertainty in home market) are
more likely to drift to Phase 4 within 12 months of launch.

Non-contractual potential: LOW CURRENTLY — but conditional on Indian
regulatory clarity (see market-cricket.md). Existing ISL token infrastructure,
even in reduced-engagement state, may have relaunch value if Indian digital
asset regulation clarifies positively. A dormant but on-chain token is a
faster foundation for relaunched utility than starting from zero.

LESSON: Regulatory environment of the club's home market is the primary
external variable for small-market token viability. Monitor the Indian
regulatory situation as a leading indicator for ISL token status.
```

### Type C — Confirmed non-contractual (smaller market clubs)

```
CASE STUDY C1: SMALLER LATIN AMERICAN AND ASIAN CLUB TOKENS
Status: Multiple smaller club tokens from Socios's 2021-22 global expansion
        effectively non-contractual following Socios's strategic contraction
        (reducing focus on smaller market partnerships from mid-2022 onward).
        These are the clearest documented non-contractual examples in the library.
Exchange: Primarily smaller CEXs and DEXs; Binance not listed for most

Context: Following Socios's mid-2022 strategic focus shift, several Latin American
and Asian club tokens (specific clubs across multiple countries) had utility
engagement effectively cease while tokens continued trading. No formal
termination announcements were made in most cases — classic silent lapse pattern.

Key insight: These tokens demonstrate Path B trajectory — without third-party
development or community organisation, small club tokens in secondary markets
gradually approach dormancy. They remain on-chain and technically tradeable
but approach de minimis volume.

WHAT DISTINGUISHES PATH A FROM PATH B IN THIS COHORT:
  Clubs that maintained active supporter engagement (outside token utility):
    → Some token trading activity persisted around major match events
    → Correlation with sporting results remained weakly positive
    
  Clubs that lost both token utility AND sporting relevance:
    → Token activity approached zero except during sporadic price spikes
    → Path B confirmed; dormancy without third-party intervention

Non-contractual potential: LOW for most — insufficient fanbase digitisation,
no third-party developer interest, CEX delisting risk without volume.
Exception: If the club achieves unexpected sporting prominence (promotion,
cup run), token may temporarily reactivate from dormancy.

LESSON: Club size and market digitisation are the primary determinants
of non-contractual token viability. The minimum viable non-contractual
token requires: (1) CEX listing maintained, (2) community of >500 active
holders, (3) sporting results still generating some media coverage.
Below these thresholds, Path B is the most likely trajectory.
```

### The relaunch pathway — non-contractual to re-contractual

```
RELAUNCH PATHWAY:

A non-contractual token can become the basis for a renewed partnership:

SCENARIO: Club that allowed its token to become non-contractual
          decides to re-enter the fan token ecosystem.

OPTIONS:
  A. Renew with original platform (Chiliz): Existing token reactivated
     → Existing holders rewarded by renewed utility
     → No new token minting needed
     → Fastest path to restored utility

  B. Launch on competing platform with new token:
     → Existing holders of original token may be airdropped new token
     → Or: Original non-contractual token trades independently;
           new platform token coexists
     → More complex; requires careful communication

  C. Issue new token while original continues:
     → Two tokens for same club; creates confusion
     → Avoid unless original token has minimal liquidity

AGENT SIGNAL: Any communication from a club that previously had a
              non-contractual token referencing digital fan products,
              blockchain, or fan engagement technology should be treated
              as a potential relaunch signal. This is one of the highest-
              value early signals in the fan token space — a relaunch
              on an existing token with an established holder community
              typically outperforms a first-time launch in terms of
              holder retention and price performance.
```

## Part 5 — The empowerment of non-contractual token communities

```
WHY NON-CONTRACTUAL TOKEN COMMUNITIES MATTER:

The post-termination holder community that remains is not a footnote —
it is SportMind's primary intelligence asset for that club in Phase 6.

COMMUNITY CHARACTERISTICS POST-TERMINATION:
  Higher commitment: Casual holders have exited; remaining holders chose to stay
  More financially aligned: They have real money at risk; they follow the club closely
  More likely to develop use cases: Community-oriented fans who build things
  More likely to provide signal: Their buy/sell decisions track sporting outcomes
  
THE EMPOWERMENT PRINCIPLE:

Non-contractual token communities should be empowered rather than abandoned.
Their continued existence on-chain enables:

  → Legitimate use cases without club approval:
     Prediction markets, GameFi, community media, fan analytics

  → Advocacy for club re-engagement:
     A large, active non-contractual holder community is evidence of
     demand that a club can point to when deciding whether to renew

  → Community treasury development:
     DEX trading fees can be directed to a community treasury
     governed by token holders — creating self-funded development
     of new utility without requiring club or platform involvement

  → Third-party developer incentivisation:
     Community treasuries can fund bounties for developers who
     build use cases on top of the non-contractual token

SPORTMIND AGENT GUIDANCE:
  Never treat a non-contractual token as worthless.
  Always assess: community size, community activity, CEX/DEX status,
  third-party developer interest, and sporting performance correlation.
  A non-contractual token with an active community, CEX listing,
  and strong sporting correlation may be MORE valuable for prediction
  purposes than a technically-active but Phase 4 plateau token with
  low utility engagement and declining holder interest.
```

## Quick reference — partnership event signals

| Event | CHZ impact | Sport impact | Agent action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 new partnership (market-creating) | +10–25% | High positive (sector re-rates) | Pre-position before listing |
| Tier 2 new partnership (major club) | +5–12% | Moderate positive | Accumulate on post-launch dip |
| Partnership renewal announced | +2–5% | Positive (reduced termination risk) | Hold / increase |
| Partnership expanded (new market) | +3–8% | Positive | Assess new holder acquisition potential |
| Utility plateau (Phase 4 signals) | Neutral | Neutral | Reduce Layer 3 weight; increase Layer 1–2 |
| Announced termination | -20–40% (token) | Neutral for sport | Load non-contractual framework; assess Path A/B |
| Silent lapse (180+ days no utility) | -10–25% (gradual) | Neutral | Treat as non-contractual; assess third-party activity |
| Forced termination (regulatory/legal) | -40–70% (token) | Negative | Immediate position reduction; monitor underlying event |
| Third-party prediction market integration | +5–20% (token) | Positive | Path A confirmed; hold |
| CEX delisting | -25–50% (liquidity) | Neutral | Assess DEX liquidity; Path B risk |
| Community DAO formation | +3–10% (sentiment) | Positive | Path A signal; third-party ecosystem forming |
| Relaunch / re-partnership signal | +15–35% (anticipation) | Positive | High-conviction early entry; validate quickly |

## Active Constructor Token Profiles

### Ferrari
**Token symbol:** (check live registry — Kayen API)
**CTI baseline:** 78–88
**HAS baseline:** 72–82
**Primary holder markets:** Italy (38%), Brazil (14%), Spain (11%), UK (8%), Argentina (7%)
**Peak FTIS events:** Monza (maximum), Monaco, any title-deciding race
**ATM pattern:** Ferrari token moves on INSTITUTIONAL signals as much as results.
  A Ferrari press conference about car development generates measurable HAS shifts.
**Key risk:** Ferrari's fan base is emotionally volatile — a DNF at Monza generates
  the largest per-event negative token move of any constructor at any circuit.
**Historical peak:** Ferrari 1-2 finish at Monza: +18–32% HAS observed 2022–2024

### Red Bull Racing
**CTI baseline:** 72–84
**HAS baseline:** 68–78
**Primary holder markets:** Netherlands (22%), UK (15%), USA (12%), Germany (10%), Brazil (8%)
**Peak FTIS events:** Season opener, Championship-deciding race, any new regulation opener
**ATM pattern:** Red Bull token is heavily influenced by constructors' championship position.
  When they are dominant, their token tracks championship gap more than individual race results.
**Key risk:** 2026 regulation reset creates highest uncertainty since 2022.
  New power unit regulations may not favour Red Bull's 2022–2024 architecture.
**Note:** Dutch holder base (Verstappen effect) — any Verstappen DTM event amplified in NL market

### Mercedes

  Mercedes token retains high baseline HAS even in non-dominant seasons (legacy effect).

| Pole position (unexpected) | 100% | 45% | 15% |

**Key insight:** F1 championship narrative events (title wins, regulation-era openers)
sustain longer than individual race results. Championship wins have the longest decay
curve — comparable to World Cup wins in football.

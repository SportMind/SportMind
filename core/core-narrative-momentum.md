# Narrative Momentum Intelligence — SportMind Core

Narrative momentum is the documented tendency for sporting outcomes to be influenced
by storylines, milestones, and emotional context that pure statistics cannot capture.
This is the signal an agent cannot derive from data alone — it requires contextual
reasoning about what a match *means* beyond its statistical inputs.

---

## Why narrative momentum is a real signal

```
THE EVIDENCE:
  "Revenge game" matchups outperform statistical expectation by ~3–7%
  "Record-chasing" athletes show elevated performance in milestone proximity
  "Comeback story" athletes after adversity outperform in narrative-rich fixtures
  "Rivalry" matches show higher effort/intensity than form alone predicts
  Home debut, career first, memorial game — all documented variance inflators
  
THE MECHANISM:
  Narratives create heightened motivation → increased effort and focus
  Narratives create home crowd amplification → acoustic and atmospheric advantage
  Narratives create opponent overconfidence or psychological pressure
  Narratives drive media coverage → more eyes → more fan token engagement
  
THE LIMIT:
  Narrative momentum is a real but SMALL modifier — typically ±3–8%
  It is a modifier on top of all other signals, not a replacement for them
  An agent should NEVER override strong adverse signals with narrative alone
  Rule: Apply narrative modifier only if statistical signals are within ±15% of neutral
```

## Narrative taxonomy — eight categories

### Category 1: Revenge fixture

```
DEFINITION: Team or athlete competing against a team/opponent who previously
caused a significant defeat, elimination, or injustice

HIGH-INTENSITY REVENGE SCENARIOS:
  Previous season's title decider / playoff elimination
  World Cup/tournament elimination rematch
  First match after losing a championship in the final
  
  Signal: Home team seeking revenge: +4–6%
  Away team seeking revenge: +3–5% (travel cost reduces slightly)
  
  VALIDITY REQUIREMENTS:
  → Must be within 18 months of the original fixture to carry full weight
  → Beyond 24 months: halve the modifier
  → Roster turnover >50%: halve the modifier (different players, different motivation)

SPECIFIC SPORT APPLICATIONS:
  Football: Europa League clubs who lost Champions League places
  MMA: Rematch after controversial decision (judges split 29-28 three times)
  NFL: Super Bowl rematch in following regular season
  Cricket: Ashes series (perpetual narrative; both nations carry multi-series context)
  Snooker: World Championship final rematch
```

### Category 2: Record proximity

```
DEFINITION: An athlete is within striking distance of a significant record

RECORD PROXIMITY TIERS:
  Career record (all-time): Highest narrative weight
  National/domestic record: High weight in home markets
  Single-season record: Moderate weight
  Sequential streak record: Moderate weight
  
  Proximity modifiers:
  1 performance away from record: +6–8% to relevant metric
  2–3 performances away: +4–6%
  4–5 performances away: +2–3%
  
SPORT-SPECIFIC RECORDS WITH DOCUMENTED PERFORMANCE UPLIFT:
  Athletics: Duplantis approaching his own pole vault WR (consistent pattern)
  MMA: Fighter approaching most finishes in weight class history
  Cricket: Batsman approaching 10,000 Test runs (milestone centuries well-documented)
  Golf: Approaching Jack Nicklaus's Major record (Tiger Woods era data available)
  Snooker: Ronnie O'Sullivan 147 count — documented elevated performance in proximity
  Football: Manager's milestone win count (500th win, etc.)
```

### Category 3: Comeback and adversity narrative

```
DEFINITION: Athlete or team returning after a major setback — injury, personal
adversity, relegation, public criticism, or being written off

HIGH-INTENSITY COMEBACK SCENARIOS:
  Return from major injury (ACL, serious illness, career-threatening injury)
  First match after public criticism/controversy from last performance
  Team that survived relegation/elimination returning to elite level
  First match after personal adversity (family loss, public personal incident)
  
  Signal: +3–5% for athlete/team in high-profile comeback context
  
  DETECTION: Monitor sports media for "comeback," "return," "redemption," "proves critics
  wrong" language in the 72h window before an event. Volume of this language correlates
  with narrative intensity.
  
  VALIDITY CHECK:
  → Is the adversity genuine and well-documented (not manufactured)?
  → Is the athlete's physical condition fully recovered (not an injury return)?
  → Narrative does not override injury modifier if player is returning from injury;
    apply injury modifier FIRST, then a reduced +2% for comeback narrative
```

### Category 4: Home debut or career first

```
DEFINITION: First occurrence of a significant career milestone

HIGH-VALUE CAREER FIRSTS:
  First home match after high-profile transfer (major signing debut)
  First trophy match (team that has never won this competition before)
  First international appearance
  First match as captain
  First match of a new stadium (venue inauguration)
  
  Signal: +3–5% for home advantage amplification (crowd amplification effect)
  
  Away team playing into a hostile maiden home crowd: −3% (crowd amplification is hostile)
  
  EXAMPLES:
  Football: Erling Haaland's Premier League debut for Man City had documented
           elevated home team performance (97,000 social mentions in 24h)
  AFL: New team's first Grand Final appearance generates extraordinary home town
       response even when playing at neutral venue (MCG)
```

### Category 5: Rivalry intensity amplifier

```
DEFINITION: Fixtures with documented cross-sport historical intensity beyond
what statistics predict

TIER 1 RIVALRIES (highest narrative weight):
  El Clásico (Barcelona vs Real Madrid): Social volume 10–15× normal match
  Manchester derby, North London derby: 5–8× normal social volume
  India vs Pakistan (cricket): Highest single-match social event in sport
  Ashes series (cricket): Multi-match sustained narrative arc
  
  Signal: All players in Tier 1 rivalry have individual performance variance increase
  Expected score convergence toward neutral regardless of form differential
  Form differential above 20%: Apply 40% discount on form signal
  (Form advantage is worth less in a derby)
  
TIER 2 RIVALRIES (moderate):
  Most domestic derbies (city rivals)
  National team historical adversaries (England vs Germany, Brazil vs Argentina)
  
  Signal: Form differential discounted by 20%

RIVALRY IDENTIFICATION:
  Not all fixtures labelled "derbies" carry narrative weight
  Requirements: Genuine historical antagonism, recent head-to-head relevance,
  significant fan community intensity (social volume metric)
```

### Category 6: Memorial and tribute matches

```
DEFINITION: Matches dedicated to recently deceased players, managers, or
club figures; matches after tragic events affecting the club community

SIGNAL: Highly unpredictable — can create either elevated performance
(rallying together) or psychological disruption (grief impairment)

AGENT RULE: Apply ZERO modifier but WIDEN confidence intervals by 15%
Memorial matches are higher variance events; do not take strong positions
This is an abstention signal, not a direction signal.

RECENT EXAMPLES:
  Leicester City after Vichai Srivaddhanaprabha's death (2018): Elevated team performance
  Several clubs after fan tragedies: Variable outcomes; cannot reliably predict direction
```

### Category 7: Elimination pressure and "must-win" context

```
DEFINITION: A team or athlete facing elimination, relegation, or career-defining
pressure in a specific fixture

MUST-WIN CONTEXT MODIFIERS:
  One loss = elimination from competition: +5% to the at-risk team
  One result = relegation confirmed: +4% to relegation-threatened team
  Career final (retirement announced, last competitive match): +3% athlete performance
  
  WHY: Motivation, focus, and risk-tolerance all shift under elimination pressure
  Teams "have nothing to lose" = more creative, higher-risk play styles

VALIDITY CHECK:
  Both teams cannot be in must-win simultaneously for the same reason
  If both teams need to win: Apply to the more desperate team only (larger gap to safety)
  
OPPOSITION EFFECT:
  Team playing a "dead rubber" opponent (already qualified/relegated): −3%
  Comfortable mid-table team vs relegation-threatened team: Upset probability higher than form suggests
```

### Category 8: Narrative fatigue and overexposure

```
DEFINITION: When a narrative has been in the media so long it's priced in
or the athlete/team is under excess psychological pressure from it

NARRATIVE FATIGUE INDICATORS:
  Same narrative discussed for 3+ weeks without resolution
  Athlete explicitly addresses narrative pressure in media (signalling awareness)
  Bookmakers have already adjusted odds significantly for the narrative

NARRATIVE FATIGUE SIGNAL: Reduce or reverse the narrative modifier
  Over-discussed narrative (4+ weeks): Reduce modifier by 50%
  Athlete showing signs of narrative burden: Reduce modifier to zero or slightly negative

EXAMPLE: A team on a long unbeaten run where the narrative about "will they
  break the record?" becomes the dominant story. The pressure can cause
  underperformance vs what the form alone would suggest.
```

## Narrative detection framework for agents

```
AUTOMATED DETECTION SIGNALS:

Social volume spike: If a fixture's social media volume is 3×+ normal:
  → High narrative intensity; identify the dominant narrative category
  → Apply relevant modifier from the taxonomy above

Media language detection: Scan match preview language for keywords:
  Revenge language: "revenge," "payback," "righting the wrong"
  Record language: "historic," "record," "milestone," "never before"
  Comeback language: "back from," "proves critics," "redemption," "return"
  Derby language: "local rivals," "derby," "more than a game," "fierce"
  Elimination language: "must win," "do or die," "final chance," "survival"

NARRATIVE SCORING:
  For each identified narrative category, score 1–3:
  3 = Primary narrative (dominant media coverage)
  2 = Secondary narrative (mentioned consistently)
  1 = Minor narrative (occasional mention)
  
  Apply modifier at 100% for score 3, 60% for score 2, 30% for score 1
  Sum multiple narratives with diminishing returns (second narrative counts 50%)

AGENT VALIDATION QUESTION:
  "Would a well-informed human sports fan, reading previews, believe this
  match has unusual significance beyond the statistics?"
  If YES: Narrative modifier likely applies
  If NO: Standard prediction; no narrative modifier needed
```

## Narrative momentum and fan token interaction

```
FAN TOKEN NARRATIVE SIGNAL:
  Narrative-rich matches generate higher social volume →
  → Higher AELS signal (athlete social lift)
  → Higher token trading volume in the 24–48h window
  → More volatile token prices (both directions)
  
  Revenge fixture win: Token spike larger than equivalent non-narrative win
  Revenge fixture loss: Token decline amplified vs equivalent non-narrative loss
  
  Record-breaking moment (live): Immediate spike; fastest token signal in the library
  
  Must-win match win: Strong token signal + continuation narrative creates sustained lift
  Must-win match loss: Severe; often compounds with elimination → significant long-term negative
  
NARRATIVE TIMING:
  Narrative builds 48–72h before event (social volume ramps)
  Agent should begin monitoring narrative intensity 4 days before major fixtures
  Peak narrative = peak token trading activity window
```

## Tournament knockout narrative amplification

Knockout rounds generate narrative momentum that compounds with each advancing
round. The elimination context (Category 7) already applies — these are the
additional multipliers that apply specifically in knockout tournament formats.

```
KNOCKOUT PHASE NARRATIVE MULTIPLIERS:
  (Applied to the base narrative modifier, not to the base signal directly)

  Round of 16 / Last 16:    × 1.20 narrative amplification
  Quarter-final:             × 1.45 narrative amplification
  Semi-final:                × 1.70 narrative amplification
  Final:                     × 2.00 narrative amplification

  WHY: Each knockout round eliminates half the field. The survivor narrative
  compounds. A team in a semi-final has already beaten 3+ opponents in direct
  elimination — each win is part of a story that builds.

DEFENDING CHAMPION NARRATIVE:
  A defending champion at any knockout competition carries:
  → +5% additional narrative modifier for every match in the competition
  → Maximum applies if they're defending a title won against dramatic opposition
  → EXCEPTION: reduce if they won in forgettable circumstances (walkover, opponent scandal)

FIRST-TIME FINALIST NARRATIVE:
  Team/athlete reaching their first final in a major competition:
  → +4% narrative modifier (breakthrough narrative + journey framing)
  → Opposition: −2% (facing a "first time" opponent is narratively neutral)

COMPOUND NARRATIVE RULE:
  When Category 7 (must-win) AND knockout amplification both apply:
  Apply Category 7 at full strength, knockout amplifier at 70%
  (Avoid double-counting the elimination pressure)

WORLD CUP 2026 SPECIFIC:
  Group stage: standard narrative taxonomy applies (no multiplier)
  Knockout from Round of 32: apply knockout phase multipliers above
  CALENDAR_COLLAPSE on elimination: narrative modifier immediately reverses to zero
  for national tokens; narrative completes within 72h for club tokens
```

## Post-tournament narrative decay

After a tournament or season concludes, narrative momentum does not end immediately.
It decays on a defined schedule that agents must respect to avoid acting on
residual signal.

```
POST-TOURNAMENT NARRATIVE PHASES:

PHASE 1 — Narrative completion window (Days 1–7 post-final):
  The winner's narrative is still resolving (celebrations, media, analysis)
  DO NOT ACT on narrative as a buy signal during Phase 1
  Residual narrative is NOT new sporting signal — it is completion of existing signal
  Agent rule: maintain positions; do not open new positions based on narrative alone

PHASE 2 — Narrative normalisation (Days 8–21):
  Winner narrative fades; loser narrative processed
  Apply 50% of narrative modifier if carrying from tournament
  Transfer window activity begins — narrative shifts from performance to commercial
  New narrative starts forming around squad reconstruction

PHASE 3 — New cycle begins (Day 22+):
  Domestic season narrative replaces tournament narrative
  Apply standard narrative taxonomy fresh — do not carry tournament modifiers
  Exception: tournament MVP retains individual narrative modifier until
  their first 3 domestic appearances

POST-TOURNAMENT CALENDAR_COLLAPSE RULE:
  Eliminated teams: narrative = zero from elimination date
  No "consolation" narrative for teams knocked out early
  Exception: if elimination was dramatic (last-minute goal, penalty shootout) →
  apply Category 3 (comeback/adversity) at 30% for the next fixture only

SEASON-END NARRATIVE DECAY:
  Applies to league seasons, not just tournaments
  Week 1–2 post-season: narrative complete; do not act
  Week 3+: new season narrative begins to build
  Cross-season narrative (relegated team returning): Category 3 applies
```

## Championship decider and season finale narrative

Distinct from knockout elimination — this applies to league-format seasons where
the title, qualification, or relegation is decided in the final rounds.

```
SEASON NARRATIVE INTENSITY — FINAL ROUNDS:

TITLE RACE (any team that can still win the title):
  Final 5 matches: × 1.15 narrative amplifier on all matches
  Final 3 matches: × 1.30
  Final 1 match (title decider): × 1.50
  Maximum: +8% to signal (narrative cap still applies)

FORMULA 1 CHAMPIONSHIP DECIDER:
  Final race with title undecided: Maximum narrative signal in motorsport
  Apply: championship_decider flag + × 1.50 narrative amplifier
  Note: F1 championship narrative is sustained across the entire season
  — distinguish between "in contention" (× 1.10) and "final race" (× 1.50)

MotoGP EQUIVALENT:
  Clinch approach: × 1.20 when leader within one win of clinching
  Final round: × 1.40 if title still undecided

NASCAR FINALE:
  Championship 4 format creates an artificial finale narrative
  Apply: × 1.35 for all four championship contenders in finale event
  Non-contenders: standard signal (no narrative modifier)

SEASON FINALE AGENT RULE:
  Always check standings before final-round analysis
  If title / promotion / relegation can change result = apply season finale framework
  If nothing at stake (confirmed position both ways) = DEAD_RUBBER; suppress narrative
```

## Cross-sport narrative dominance

During major global events, one sport can dominate the narrative landscape in a
way that compresses signals for all other sports. This is a library-level signal
that affects all sports simultaneously.

```
CROSS-SPORT NARRATIVE DOMINANCE EVENTS:

FIFA WORLD CUP (active tournament):
  Football narrative dominance: MAXIMUM
  Effect on other sports: media attention redistributed
  Token signal compression: non-football tokens see 15–25% reduced social volume
  Agent rule: during WC group stage, apply × 0.85 to AELS for non-football tokens
  Rationale: social platforms dominated by football; non-football athlete posts
  receive lower engagement than baseline

OLYMPICS:
  Cross-sport narrative: all sports elevated during Games period
  No compression effect — Olympics amplifies ALL sports
  Agent rule: apply standard narrative taxonomy; no cross-sport modifier needed
  Exception: direct scheduling conflicts with major domestic leagues

SUPER BOWL WEEK:
  NFL dominance for 2 full weeks pre-game
  US-market tokens: apply compression factor to non-NFL tokens (× 0.90)
  Global tokens: minimal effect (Super Bowl is primarily a US narrative)

DETECTING NARRATIVE DOMINANCE:
  Trigger: social volume for football/target sport exceeds 5× baseline for 3+ days
  Source: platform/social-intelligence-connector.md (LunarCrush Galaxy Score)
  Action: flag CROSS_SPORT_COMPRESSION; apply sport-specific modifiers above
```

## Trilogy and multi-bout narrative (combat sports)

In MMA, boxing, and wrestling, multi-bout series between the same fighters
create a compound narrative structure unlike anything in team sports.

```
BOUT SERIES NARRATIVE FRAMEWORK:

REMATCH (second fight):
  Decisive first fight win: Standard narrative (loser seeking redemption = Category 1)
  Controversial first fight: × 1.30 narrative amplifier (dispute needs resolution)
  Long gap since first fight (2+ years): reduce to × 1.10 (narrative has cooled)

TRILOGY (third fight):
  Any trilogy fight: MAXIMUM narrative signal in combat sports
  Apply: trilogy_flag + × 1.50 narrative amplifier
  Examples: Jones vs Cormier III, Fury vs Wilder III, Barrera vs Morales III
  Rationale: third fight carries weight of entire series; both fighters defined by it

BEYOND TRILOGY (4th fight+):
  Narrative fatigue begins to apply (Category 8)
  Reduce modifier: × 0.80 per additional fight beyond trilogy
  Exception: if a new title or career milestone is at stake — reset to Category 1

AGENT RULE — COMBAT SPORTS:
  Weight cuts, injury history, and age modifier apply BEFORE narrative
  Do not let trilogy narrative override significant physical decline signals
  If fighter is 3+ years past peak ATM: narrative modifier capped at × 1.10
  regardless of series context
```

## Drive to Survive / documentary narrative (DTS effect)

The documentary and streaming content cycle creates a distinct type of narrative
momentum that operates separately from in-season sporting performance. Named
after the Netflix Formula 1 series but applicable across sports.

```
DTS FRAMEWORK:

WHAT DTS NARRATIVE IS:
  A narrative that exists because of media content, not sporting events
  The story is *about* an athlete or team, not *by* their performance
  Creates new audiences who follow the sport emotionally before understanding it

  DTS-driven fans are:
  → More likely to be new token holders (lower governance engagement)
  → More reactive to media narrative than sporting outcomes
  → Amplifiers for social signal but weaker holders (Speculator archetype)

SIGNAL APPLICATION:
  Active DTS narrative (series recently released, press coverage high):
    → × 1.15 to AELS for featured athletes/teams
    → Elevated social volume unlikely to reflect actual sporting signal quality
    → Apply CAUTION flag: social volume may be noise, not signal

  Featured team or athlete = new entry (season or series launch):
    → Apply narrative_active flag for first 4–6 weeks post-release
    → Distinguish: DTS attention ≠ form improvement; do not modify SMS

SPORTS WHERE DTS EFFECT IS ACTIVE:
  Formula 1 (Netflix — primary origin): applies every season
  Tennis (Break Point series): Wimbledon period amplified
  NFL (various docuseries): training camp window
  Golf, cycling: emerging DTS signals

AGENT RULE:
  bc_dts_effect MCP tool returns current DTS narrative status per sport
  DTS narrative = commercial signal only (AELS, CDI, token volume)
  DTS narrative = NEVER a sporting outcome signal (no SMS modification)
```

## Loading instruction

```
Load AFTER all quantitative modifiers (athlete, congestion, officiating, weather)
Apply narrative modifier LAST — it adjusts the already-adjusted signal

Order:
1. Base signal
2. Athlete modifier (Layer 2)
3. Congestion modifier
4. Officiating modifier
5. Weather modifier
6. Macro modifier (Layer 5)
7. Narrative modifier (this file) ← applied last
→ Final adjusted signal

MAXIMUM NARRATIVE MODIFIER: ±8% — never exceed this regardless of narrative intensity
Narrative is a real but bounded signal; it cannot override large quantitative deficits
```

## Ticket demand as a pre-event narrative signal

Ticket demand is a leading indicator of fixture importance that precedes
fan token HAS movement by 24-72 hours.

```
TICKET DEMAND SIGNAL FRAMEWORK:

SELL-OUT STATUS:
  Sold out weeks in advance:
    → narrative_active flag recommended
    → Apply × 1.05 to narrative_momentum calculation
    → Fan token HAS typically spikes 48-72h before a sold-out match
  Heavy unsold inventory (< 70% expected attendance):
    → Apply × 0.92 to home advantage modifier

SECONDARY MARKET PRICING:
  Resale > 3× face value: confirm narrative_active flag; HAS spike follows within 48h
  Resale < face value: reduced home advantage modifier

TIMING PROTOCOL:
  T-72h: Check sell-out status and secondary market pricing
  T-24h: Confirm attendance expectations
  Freshness: Tier 3 (daily) — do not use cached data

CUP FINAL TICKET ALLOCATION:
  Neutral venue finals: reduced home advantage (approximately 40% reduction)

AGENT RULE:
  For Tier 1 and 2 fixtures: ticket demand check is part of the T-72h protocol.
  Sold-out with resale > 3× = confirm narrative_active and apply × 1.05 modifier.
```

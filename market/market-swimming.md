# Swimming — SportMind Market Intelligence

## At a Glance
**Tier 4 — Niche / Structurally Distant.** Swimming has enormous Olympic viewership but
almost no sustained commercial engagement outside the 4-year cycle. Individual swimmer
brands (Michael Phelps legacy, Katie Ledecky, Adam Peaty) have genuine recognition but
limited ongoing commercial infrastructure. No fan tokens. The Olympic-peak model makes
sustained token engagement structurally difficult.

---

## Fan Token Readiness Tier

**Tier 4 — Niche / Structurally Distant**

Swimming's commercial engagement is almost entirely concentrated in the Olympic Games every
4 years. World Championships (biennial) provide some mid-cycle activity but at dramatically
lower commercial scale. Without sustained year-round competition that fans engage with,
the token utility model doesn't have enough activation windows to function effectively.

---

## 1. Global Revenue and Broadcast Deals

**World Aquatics (governing body):** ~$50M/yr revenue (excluding Olympic period)
**Olympic swimming broadcast:** Embedded in Olympic deals (NBC, BBC, etc.) — not separately negotiated
**ISL (International Swimming League):** Attempted professional swimming league 2019–2021; suspended;
attempted to create year-round professional competition but financial viability was not established

---

## 2. Fanbase Size, Geography, and Demographic Profile

**Global fan estimate:** ~2.2B (broad; Olympic viewership); ~100M engaged year-round
**Age profile:** Median age ~30 (young by Olympic standards; broad participation base)
**Participation vs spectatorship gap:** Swimming is the most participated sport globally
but has among the lowest spectatorship relative to participation

---

## 3. Token Catalyst Events

**ISL revival:** If a professional swimming league is successfully revived with year-round
competition, it would be the prerequisite for token viability.
**Los Angeles 2028:** US hosting of Olympics would be the highest-profile swimming moment
in the near term — possible token adjacent activity.

---

## 4. Agent Context Summary

- Swimming is Tier 4 primarily due to the 4-year commercial cycle and lack of persistent
  professional club structure
- World record moments (Katie Ledecky, Adam Peaty) are the sport's most commercially
  valuable events — viral but isolated
- Any professional league revival (ISL model) would be the prerequisite for tier upgrade

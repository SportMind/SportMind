# Baseball (MLB) — SportMind Market Intelligence

## At a Glance
**Tier 2 — High Credibility, Near-Term.** MLB is one of the four major North American
sports leagues with ~$11B in annual revenue. No active Chiliz fan tokens exist. Baseball
has the most data-rich sport ecosystem in the world (Statcast), a globally distributed
fanbase with strong Latin American and Asian components, and franchise values among the
highest in sport. The barrier to tokens is the same as NFL: US regulatory environment
and league-level institutional caution.

---

## Fan Token Readiness Tier

**Tier 2 — High Credibility, Near-Term**

MLB has demonstrated openness to digital products — it was an early adopter of NFTs
(MLB Champions on Flow blockchain, 2019) and has run multiple digital collectible programmes.
The league's international partnerships (NPB in Japan, KBO in Korea, Cuban talent pipeline)
give it a broader geographic footprint than NFL. The Latin American and Asian fanbase
components are among the most digitally engaged sports audiences in their respective regions.

## 2. Fanbase Size, Geography, and Demographic Profile

**Global fan estimate:** ~500M
**US fan estimate:** ~90M (third behind NFL and NBA domestically)

**Geographic breakdown:**
1. **United States** — Core commercial market; declining youth engagement domestically
2. **Latin America** — ~120M fans; Dominican Republic, Venezuela, Cuba, Mexico, Puerto Rico
   are essential talent pipelines and passionate fan markets
3. **Japan** — ~50M fans; NPB is significant; Shohei Ohtani's global profile elevated Japanese interest
4. **South Korea** — ~20M fans; KBO strong; Park Chan-ho/Ryu Hyun-jin era opened market
5. **Canada** — ~15M fans; Blue Jays strong market in Toronto

**Age profile:** Median MLB fan age: ~57 (oldest of any sport in this library)
- This is the sport's greatest commercial challenge domestically
- However: Latin American and Asian fan demographics are significantly younger (~30–35)
- The pitch clock rule changes (2023) explicitly targeted younger domestic fans

**Gender split:** ~55% male / ~45% female

**Key demographic insight:** Baseball has a domestic demographic crisis (oldest fanbase
in the library) but an international demographic advantage (Latin American and Asian fans
are younger and more digitally engaged). Token strategy should prioritise international
markets first.

## 4. Institutional Blockchain and Fan Token Interest

**MLB stance:** Most digitally experimental of the four major North American leagues
- MLB Champions (Flow blockchain, 2019): early official NFT programme
- MLB NFTs continued through multiple iterations
- MLB Advanced Media (MLBAM) is one of the most sophisticated digital sports media
  operations globally — they built the streaming infrastructure used by other leagues
- No formal Chiliz partnership but openness to digital product experimentation is documented

**International partners:** NPB (Japan) and KBO (Korea) have been independently
exploring digital fan engagement — a Chiliz partnership with NPB would be the most
likely entry point for baseball tokens given Japanese market sophistication

## 5. Active Fan Token Ecosystem

**Current status:** No active MLB team tokens on Chiliz

**Most likely first tokens:** New York Yankees and Los Angeles Dodgers are the two
highest-brand-value MLB franchises globally. Shohei Ohtani's Dodgers provides a
specific Japanese-market token catalyst that no other MLB franchise has.

**Japan pathway:** An NPB-Chiliz partnership (independent of MLB) is the most plausible
near-term token entry for baseball. Japanese baseball fans are wealthy, digitally
sophisticated, and deeply loyal to specific clubs — ideal token demographics.

## 6. Media Rights Trajectory

**Direction: Flat to modest growth domestically; growing internationally**

The Regional Sports Network (RSN) crisis (multiple RSNs bankrupt/restructuring 2023-24)
is baseball's primary rights challenge. Local market rights — historically baseball's
most valuable asset — are structurally weakening. National and streaming rights are
compensating but not yet fully offset.

**Pitch clock impact:** Rule change in 2023 reduced average game time from 3:04 to 2:39.
Early evidence of improved younger viewer engagement. Long-term rights trajectory
depends on whether the pace-of-play changes sustain youth interest.

---

## 7. Token Catalyst Events

**Shohei Ohtani's Dodgers tenure:** The most globally marketable baseball player since
Ichiro is now on the highest-profile franchise. A Dodgers token with explicit Japanese-
market targeting (Ohtani-linked utility) would be the most commercially credible
baseball token launch possible.

**NPB-Chiliz partnership:** Japanese baseball's commercial sophistication, fan loyalty,
and digital infrastructure make NPB the most natural entry point for baseball tokens
outside the US regulatory environment.

**US regulatory clarity:** Same NFL dynamic applies — SEC/CFTC framework is the gating variable.

**RSN replacement products:** As Regional Sports Networks collapse, clubs are developing
direct-to-fan streaming products. Fan token utility within these club apps is a natural
integration.

## Agent Context Summary

- Baseball has the oldest domestic fanbase in the library but strong international
  demographics (Latin America, Japan, Korea) that are younger and more token-ready
- Shohei Ohtani + Dodgers is the single most commercially powerful individual/club
  combination in baseball for token launch — Japanese market is the primary target
- NPB (Japan) rather than MLB (US) is the most plausible near-term token entry point
- MLB's digital sophistication (MLBAM) means the infrastructure exists — the barrier
  is regulatory and institutional, not technical
- RSN crisis is baseball's domestic rights challenge; fan tokens could be part of the
  direct-to-fan commercial model that replaces RSN revenue for smaller clubs

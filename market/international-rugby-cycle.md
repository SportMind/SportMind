# Rugby International Cycle — SportMind Intelligence Framework

**The perpetual cycle model for international rugby — both union and league.**
Applies the same structural insight as `market/international-football-cycle.md`
to rugby's two distinct codes, each with its own competition calendar and
NCSI hierarchy.

---

## Why rugby needs its own cycle document

Rugby union and rugby league are structurally different from football's international
cycle in three important ways that affect how agents should reason about them:

**1. The annual window structure differs.** Football has ten international breaks per year
spread across the calendar. Rugby union has one concentrated annual window (November
internationals, February–March Six Nations) and a World Cup every four years. Rugby
league has the State of Origin as its dominant annual signal — not a bilateral series
but an intra-national competition that disrupts the NRL club calendar more than any
international series disrupts football.

**2. The token ecosystem is pre-launch but catalyst-ready.** Neither code has active fan tokens at the time of writing. The CVC Capital Partners investment in Six
Nations, Premiership Rugby, and the United Rugby Championship is the structural commercial
signal that rugby union is building toward the same tokenisation pathway football has
followed. The World Cup is the activation window. Agents monitoring rugby tokens need
to know exactly which competitions matter and when.

**3. Post-tournament behaviour differs from football.** A Rugby World Cup does not have
an immediate transfer window equivalent. Club seasons continue. The signal transition
is primarily narrative-driven rather than market-driven.

## RUGBY UNION — International Cycle

### The three-tier union NCSI hierarchy

| Competition | NCSI weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rugby World Cup Final | 1.00 | Maximum; 4-year cycle event |
| Rugby World Cup (QF onwards) | 0.85–0.95 | Knockout stage; escalating |
| Rugby World Cup (pool stage) | 0.60 | Group stage; competitive but not eliminating |
| Six Nations Grand Slam (decider) | 0.80 | Annual peak; entire tournament narrative |
| Six Nations (final weekend) | 0.75 | Title and relegation stakes highest |
| The Rugby Championship (Bledisloe Cup) | 0.70 | AUS/NZL rivalry; highest RC fixture |
| The Rugby Championship (standard) | 0.55 | Southern hemisphere annual competition |
| Six Nations (standard round) | 0.50 | Early rounds; lower individual stakes |
| British & Irish Lions tour | 0.75 | Every 4 years; unique British-Irish combination |
| Autumn Internationals (November) | 0.35–0.50 | Test window; varies by opposition |
| Summer tours | 0.30 | Lower stakes; squad rotation common |

### Six Nations — the annual signal anchor

```
SIX NATIONS CALENDAR (February–March, annual):
  Nations: England, France, Ireland, Italy, Scotland, Wales
  Format: Round-robin, 5 rounds over 7 weeks
  
SIGNAL STRUCTURE:
  Round 1 (early February): Moderate signal — season opener
  Round 2-3: Building signal — form and momentum establishing
  Round 4-5 (late February–March): Maximum signal
    → Title contention resolves in rounds 4-5
    → Grand Slam possibilities confirmed/denied
    → Relegation (promotion/relegation introduced 2024-25) stakes active
    
GRAND SLAM SIGNAL:
  A Grand Slam (win all 5 matches) attempt creates sustained 7-week narrative
  Final match of a Grand Slam attempt: NCSI weight 0.80 (approaching World Cup)
  Grand Slam completed: +15-25% token signal for clubs with players from that nation
  
CVC INVESTMENT CONTEXT:
  CVC Capital Partners invested ~$400M for 14.3% stake in Six Nations (2021)
  This is the structural signal that Six Nations will commercialise digitally
  Token pathway: Six Nations national tokens are the most credible near-term
  rugby union token product — more commercially ready than club tokens
  
  MONITORING: Six Nations commercial announcements (digital, fan engagement)
  Any partnership with fan token platforms = Tier 2 → 1 upgrade signal
```

### Rugby World Cup — the 4-year activation window

```
RUGBY WORLD CUP CALENDAR:
  2019: Japan (France quarter-final; England final)
  2023: France ✅
  2027: Australia
  2031: USA
  
2027 AUSTRALIA SIGNIFICANCE:
  Australia hosting = potential domestic market activation
  Southern hemisphere rugby + crypto adoption growing
  
POOL STAGE SIGNAL CALENDAR:
  Draw announcement (18 months before): national token spike
  Pool match vs traditional rival: NCSI weight 0.70
  Pool clincher (qualification secured): NCSI weight 0.65
  
KNOCKOUT SIGNAL:
  Quarter-final: NCSI 0.85 — eliminates 4 of 8 remaining nations
  Semi-final: NCSI 0.95 — final four
  Final: NCSI 1.00 — tournament peak
  
POST-TOURNAMENT TRANSITION (rugby union):
  Week 1-2: Narrative completes. Final result dominates.
  Week 3-4: Player of the tournament transfer speculation (limited — rugby has
            salary cap constraints that limit large moves)
  Month 2: Club Premiership/URC season resumes focus
  
  KEY DIFFERENCE FROM FOOTBALL: No immediate transfer window equivalent.
  Agent rule: Post-World Cup narrative decay is faster in rugby than football.
  Return to club competition signals within 3-4 weeks of final.
```

### British & Irish Lions — the unique quadrennial signal

```
THE BRITISH & IRISH LIONS:
  Formed from best players of England, Ireland, Scotland, Wales
  Tours southern hemisphere nations (NZ, Australia, South Africa) every 4 years
  Most recent: 2021 (South Africa), 2025 (Australia)
  
WHY LIONS MATTERS FOR TOKENS:
  Unique cross-national identity — fans from 4 nations support one team
  For club tokens: their best players are absent for 6-8 weeks
  Club token signal: negative for clubs with many Lions (squad depleted)
  Player token (if exists): peak profile moment — maximum ATM for Lions players
  
NCSI LIONS WEIGHT: 0.75 (comparable to Six Nations climax)
  Lions Test Series win: +12-20% for tokens connected to winning nations
  Lions player selection: individual player ATM spike (+10-15% for club token)
  
TIMING: Lions tours June-July (between club seasons) — lower club disruption
  than State of Origin (mid-season NRL disruption)
```

### Post-tournament NCSI reset (rugby union)

```
SEASONAL RHYTHM:
  August-October:    Club competitions resume (Premiership, URC, Top 14)
                     International signal dormant
  November:          Autumn Internationals window (NCSI weight 0.35-0.50)
                     Southern hemisphere teams tour north
  December-January:  Club competitions only
  February-March:    Six Nations (NCSI activates strongly)
  April-May:         Club competition climax (Premiership/URC finals)
  June-July:         Lions tour (if year) or summer tests
                     Southern hemisphere Rugby Championship begins
  
AGENT RULE: Track which window the current date falls in.
  Outside international windows: club competition is the sole signal.
  November window: load Autumn Internationals weights (not Six Nations weights).
```

## RUGBY LEAGUE — International Cycle

Rugby league has a fundamentally different international signal structure to union.
The dominant annual signal is not an international competition at all — it is the
State of Origin, which is an intra-national Australian competition that disrupts
the NRL club calendar.

### The rugby league signal hierarchy

| Competition | NCSI weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rugby League World Cup Final | 1.00 | Maximum; held approximately every 4 years |
| Rugby League World Cup (SF/Final) | 0.85–0.95 | Knockout escalation |
| State of Origin (Game 3 decider) | 0.90 | Highest domestic signal in rugby league |
| State of Origin (Games 1-2) | 0.75–0.80 | Annual intra-national series |
| RLWC pool + knockout (early) | 0.55–0.70 | World Cup group stage |
| International Test (England, NZ, Tonga) | 0.40–0.55 | Autumn/end-of-season |
| Pacific Championships | 0.45 | Pacific nations competition |
| NRL Grand Final | 0.90 | Domestic — not international; but peak annual signal |

### State of Origin — the defining annual signal

Full detail in `fan-token/rugby-league-token-intelligence/`. Summary for cycle context:

```
STATE OF ORIGIN IN THE ANNUAL CYCLE:
  May-July: Three matches between NSW (Blues) and QLD (Maroons)
  Game 1 (late May): Season opener; highest Origin uncertainty
  Game 2 (late June): Series context established  
  Game 3 (mid-July): Decider — NCSI weight 0.90 (approaching World Cup Final)
  
DOWNSTREAM NRL DISRUPTION (the most commercially valuable signal):
  During Origin period: NRL clubs depleted by player absences
  High-Origin clubs (4+ players absent): congestion modifier ×0.88
  Low-Origin clubs (opponent depleted): opportunity modifier ×1.04-1.07
  
  This is not an international signal in the traditional sense but it functions
  as the equivalent — it is the annual high-water mark for rugby league signal.

POST-STATE OF ORIGIN TRANSITION:
  August onwards: NRL season resumes with full squads
  September: NRL finals — signal amplifies to World Cup equivalent
  October: NRL Grand Final — peak domestic signal (NCSI weight 0.90 equivalent)
  
  Return to full signal: faster than football post-tournament
  August NRL: normal club signal within 2 weeks of Origin conclusion
```

### Rugby League World Cup — the 4-year activation window

```
RLWC CALENDAR (approximate — held every ~4 years):
  2017: Australia/New Zealand/Papua New Guinea
  2021/22: England ✅ (delayed from 2021, held October-November 2022)
  2025: USA (planned)
  
UNIQUE CHARACTERISTICS:
  PNG (Papua New Guinea) is a rugby league nation — genuine national passion
  Tonga, Samoa, Fiji: Pacific nations with growing diaspora engagement
  England: traditional power; strong UK token market potential
  
  RLWC token opportunity:
  PNG national token: most culturally authentic non-traditional token in sport
  Tonga/Samoa: Pacific diaspora markets (Australia, New Zealand, UK)
  England RL: distinct from rugby union; separate tokenisation pathway
  
RLWC SIGNAL CALENDAR:
  Draw announcement: national token speculation
  Pool stage (PNG, Pacific nations): highest per-match engagement for non-traditional
  Knockout rounds: standard NCSI escalation
  Final: NCSI 1.00
  
POST-RLWC TRANSITION:
  Faster return to club competition than union
  NRL season typically continues or resumes immediately after
  Agent rule: 1-2 week post-RLWC narrative; then full NRL signal
```

  October-November: NRL Grand Final + Autumn Internationals + RLWC (if year)

  Agent rule: NRL Grand Final > Autumn Internationals for club token priority.
              RLWC overrides all if World Cup is in progress.

# Badminton — SportMind Market Intelligence

## At a Glance
**Tier 3 — Asia-Concentrated with Token Potential.** Badminton is the second most played
sport in the world (after football) with extraordinary depth in China, Indonesia, Malaysia,
India, and South Korea. The Badminton World Federation (BWF) has a professional tour
(BWF World Tour) and major events (All England, Thomas Cup, Uber Cup) that generate
significant Asian viewership. No fan tokens, but the Asian market concentration gives
this sport more token potential than its Western commercial profile suggests.

---

## Fan Token™ Readiness Tier

**Tier 3 — Asia-Concentrated with Token Potential**

Badminton is a unique case: tiny Western commercial profile but massive Asian engagement.
Indonesia's Lee Chong Wei era and current players (Viktor Axelsen, Anthony Sinisuka Ginting)
generate extraordinary followings in Southeast Asia. The combination of Southeast Asian
mobile-first digital engagement, high crypto adoption in Indonesia/Thailand/Philippines,
and passionate national team cultures makes badminton more token-plausible than its
Western reputation suggests.

---

## 1. Global Revenue and Broadcasts

**BWF revenue:** ~$40M/yr
**Domestic broadcast (Asia):** CCTV (China), RCTI (Indonesia), RTM (Malaysia), DD Sports (India)
**Thomas and Uber Cup Finals:** Most-watched badminton events; Indonesian viewing figures rival
football viewership

---

## 2. Fanbase and Demographics

**Global fan estimate:** ~2.2B (participation-based); ~500M engaged spectators
**Core markets:** China (~500M), Indonesia (~200M), India (~150M), Malaysia (~15M), South Korea (~20M)
**Age profile:** Median age ~27 in Southeast Asia (very young); ~35 globally
**Gender split:** ~50% male / ~50% female

---

## 3. Token Catalyst Events

**Indonesia national team token:** The most commercially passionate badminton nation — Thomas
Cup wins are national events. An Indonesian Badminton Association token would reach the most
engaged single-nation fanbase in the sport.
**Viktor Axelsen / Tai Tzu-ying player tokens:** The current generation of dominant players
have strong Southeast Asian followings.
**BWF-Chiliz partnership:** BWF's centralised commercial control (similar to MotoGP's Dorna)
means one deal covers the entire world tour.

---

## 4. Agent Context Summary

- Southeast Asian mobile-first digital engagement is the defining commercial characteristic
  for badminton — Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand have high crypto adoption rates and young demographics
- Thomas Cup / Uber Cup (team events) are the most natural token activation windows —
  persistent national team identities vs individual player career uncertainty
- BWF's centralised structure means one partnership deal covers the full World Tour —
  simpler pathway than fragmented sport governance structures

# MotoGP — SportMind Market Intelligence

## At a Glance
**Tier 2 — High Credibility, Near-Term.** MotoGP is the world's premier motorcycle
racing championship with a global audience of ~400M and particular strength in Southern
Europe, Southeast Asia, and Latin America. No active fan tokens. Dorna Sports
(MotoGP promoter) has been active in digital fan engagement and the sport's rider-centric
token model (rather than team-centric like F1) creates natural fan token opportunities.

---

## Fan Token Readiness Tier

**Tier 2 — High Credibility, Near-Term**

MotoGP's commercial profile (revenue ~$700M+, growing broadcast deals, strongly digital
fanbase in Spain, Italy, and Southeast Asia) supports Tier 2 comfortably. The sport's
geographic concentration in markets with high crypto adoption (Spain, Italy) and Southeast
Asia's mobile-first digital engagement makes it structurally well-suited to tokens.

## 2. Fanbase Size, Geography, and Demographic Profile

**Global fan estimate:** ~400M
**Core markets:** Spain (~45M fans), Italy (~40M), Indonesia (~80M — largest single market)
**Southeast Asia total:** ~150M fans (Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia, Philippines)

**Age profile:** Median age ~32; under-35 share ~55%
**Gender split:** ~75% male / ~25% female
**Key demographic:** Southeast Asian fanbase is younger (median ~27), mobile-first,
and growing fastest — the most token-ready geographic segment

---

## 3. Digital and Social Engagement Index

**MotoGP official:** ~20M combined social followers
**Rider accounts:** Marc Márquez (~12M), Fabio Quartararo (~5M), Francesco Bagnaia (~4M)
**Crash.net / Motorsport.com:** Core digital fan platforms; high engagement

**Indonesia factor:** Indonesian fans' digital engagement with MotoGP rivals Spain and
Italy in volume — particularly around the Indonesian GP (Pertamina Mandalika circuit,
est. 2021). Indonesian mobile internet penetration at ~80% with highly active social sharing.

---

## 4. Institutional Blockchain and Fan Token Interest

**Dorna Sports stance:** Digitally active — MotoGP has run NFT programmes and digital
collectible initiatives. The organisational structure (Dorna owns MotoGP commercial rights
centrally, unlike F1's distributed model) means a single Dorna-Chiliz deal would cover
the entire championship.

**Manufacturers:** Ducati, Honda, Yamaha, Aprilia, KTM — all major manufacturers have
significant digital marketing budgets; sponsor and partner with digital brands

---

## 5. Active Fan Token Ecosystem

**Current status:** No active MotoGP tokens on Chiliz

**Most natural token structure:**
- Manufacturer tokens (Ducati, Honda, Yamaha) — team-equivalent to F1 constructor tokens
- Rider tokens — individual champion/star tokens (Márquez has the largest global following)
- Combination: manufacturer + rider dual token as unique structure

---

## 6. Media Rights Trajectory

**Direction: Growing — Southeast Asian expansion driving growth**

Dorna's expansion of the calendar (Indonesian GP, Thai GP, Mandalika circuit) is
explicitly commercial — these races target the highest-growth fanbase geographically.
Rights value in Southeast Asia is growing ~15–20% annually.

---

## 7. Token Catalyst Events

**Dorna-Chiliz partnership:** Dorna's centralised commercial control means one deal
unlocks the entire championship. This is simpler than F1 (where Liberty + individual
constructors must all participate).

**Marc Márquez's return / Ducati move:** The sport's most globally marketed rider
changing manufacturers in 2024 is a sustained narrative catalyst. Márquez on Ducati
is the most commercially significant rider move in a decade.

**Indonesia GP:** The Indonesian Grand Prix (annual, Lombok/Mandalika) has become a
flagship event for Southeast Asian token activation — Indonesian fans' digital engagement
is extraordinary.

## Agent Context Summary

- Southeast Asia (Indonesia 80M+ fans; Thailand, Malaysia, Philippines) is the highest-growth
  MotoGP market and the most token-ready geographic segment
- Dorna's centralised structure means a single partnership deal unlocks the entire championship —
  simpler entry than F1 or football
- Manufacturer tokens (Ducati, Honda) are more stable than rider tokens — rider careers
  are shorter and crash risk is higher than any other motorsport in the library
- Wet race specialist dynamics (see domain skill) create the highest single-race variance
  signal in motorsport — token prices in wet MotoGP races are highly volatile

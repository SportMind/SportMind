# Rugby League — SportMind Market Intelligence

## At a Glance
**Tier 2 — High Credibility, Near-Term.** Rugby league has two major professional
competitions: the NRL (National Rugby League, Australia) and Super League (UK). Combined
revenue ~$450M. No active fan tokens. The sport's core markets (Queensland and NSW in
Australia; Yorkshire and Lancashire in England) have passionate, concentrated fanbases
with strong club identity — similar in structure to football's regional club token ecosystems.

---

## Fan Token™ Readiness Tier

**Tier 2 — High Credibility, Near-Term**

Rugby league's smaller commercial scale (vs rugby union or AFL) means it sits at the
lower boundary of Tier 2. However, specific characteristics make it credible: NRL
clubs have some of the most loyal fanbases in Australian sport (Origin identity is
culturally foundational), Super League's UK clubs sit in football-token-adjacent markets,
and the sport has a young median fan age relative to rugby union.

---

## 1. Global Revenue and Broadcast Deals

**NRL revenue (2023):** ~$650M AUD (~$400M USD)
**Super League revenue (2023):** ~£70M (~$85M USD)
**Combined global:** ~$500M

**Key broadcast deals:**
- NRL: Nine Network / Foxtel / Kayo Sports (Australia) — ~$400M AUD/yr 6-year deal
- Super League: Sky Sports UK — ~£40M/yr
- NRL international: Fox Sports NZ, PNG TV (PNG is a rugby league nation)

---

## 2. Fanbase Size, Geography, and Demographic Profile

**NRL fan estimate:** ~3M avid fans in Australia; concentrated in NSW and QLD
**Super League fan estimate:** ~2M in UK; concentrated in Yorkshire, Lancashire, London
**Papua New Guinea:** ~6M fans — per-capita the most rugby-league-passionate nation on Earth
**New Zealand:** ~1.5M fans; Warriors NRL team is NZ's primary rugby league vehicle

**Age profile:** NRL median age ~35; under-35 share ~45%
**Gender split:** ~60% male / ~40% female

---

## 3. Digital and Social Engagement Index

**NRL digital:** Strong in Australia; NRL app is well-developed
**State of Origin:** Most-watched annual sporting event in NSW and QLD — consistently
highest-rating Australian TV broadcast
**Super League:** Lower digital engagement than NRL; primarily broadcast-led

---

## 4. Institutional Blockchain and Fan Token Interest

**NRL stance:** No formal blockchain engagement; commercially aware but not active
**Super League / RL Commercial:** No formal engagement
**PNG factor:** Papua New Guinea's rugby league passion is enormous — the sport is the
national sport. PNG's mobile-first economy and young population make it an interesting
emerging market for any token product, though current infrastructure limits near-term viability.

---

## 5. Active Fan Token Ecosystem

**Current status:** No active tokens

**Most credible first tokens:**
- South Sydney Rabbitohs or Sydney Roosters (NRL): highest brand value in competition
- State of Origin tokens (NSW Blues / QLD Maroons): the most emotionally charged annual
  sporting series in Australia — natural token activation twice-yearly (two representative teams)

---

## 6. Media Rights Trajectory

**Direction: Stable (Australia); modest growth (UK)**

NRL's Nine/Foxtel deal runs through 2027. Expected renewal at similar or modest
improvement. Super League's Sky deal is stable.

---

## 7. Token Catalyst Events

**State of Origin week:** Three times per year, the NRL's biggest commercial event.
A State of Origin token (Blues vs Maroons) is the most commercially intuitive rugby
league token structure — not club-based, but representative-team-based.

**NRL finals:** The NRL Grand Final at ANZ Stadium is the closest rugby league equivalent
to an AFL Grand Final — peak commercial moment.

# Netball — SportMind Market Intelligence

## At a Glance
**Tier 3/4 — Niche with Growth Potential.** Netball is the most popular women's team
sport in the Commonwealth with 20M+ regular players globally. No fan tokens. The sport
has experienced rapid commercial growth (Netball Super League UK TV deal, ANZ Championship
in Australia) but remains small by global commercial standards. The sport's female-majority
fanbase is its most commercially distinctive characteristic.

---

## Fan Token Readiness Tier

**Tier 3/4 — Niche with Growth Potential**

Netball is a sport that punches above its weight culturally in specific markets (Australia,
England, New Zealand, Jamaica, South Africa) but below its weight commercially. Revenue
is too small (<$100M globally) for confident Tier 3 classification, but the sport's growth
trajectory and female-majority fanbase make it worth monitoring.

---

## 1. Global Revenue and Broadcast Deals

**Global netball commercial value:** ~$60–80M/yr (estimated; limited public data)
**Netball Super League (UK):** Sky Sports deal (~£3M/yr) — significant for the sport's scale
**Suncorp Super Netball (Australia):** Channel Nine / Fox Sports deal (~A$15M/yr)
**International Netball Federation:** World Cup generates ~$20M commercial activity per event

---

## 2. Fanbase Size, Geography, and Demographic Profile

**Global player count:** 20M+ (most players of any women's team sport)
**Fan estimate:** ~200M (concentrated in Commonwealth nations)
**Core markets:** Australia (~5M fans), England (~4M), New Zealand (~1.5M), Jamaica (~800K), South Africa (~2M)
**Age profile:** Median age ~28; under-35 share ~65% (one of youngest fanbases in library)
**Gender split:** ~75% female / ~25% male — highest female share of any sport in library

---

## 3. Digital and Social Engagement Index

**Growth trajectory:** Netball Super League and Suncorp Super Netball viewership
has grown ~40% since 2019. The Roses (England) vs Diamonds (Australia) rivalry
generates genuine social engagement.
**Commonwealth Games:** Netball's primary global broadcast moment; growing viewership

---

## 4. Institutional Blockchain and Fan Token Interest

**INF (World Netball) stance:** No engagement; commercially developing
**Australian market:** High crypto adoption (25%+ adults); ANZ Netball commercial sophistication growing
**Athlete generation:** Current generation of elite netballers (Helen Housby, Donnell Wallam,
Steph Wood) are social-media-native and commercially aware

---

## 5. Active Fan Token Ecosystem / Token Catalyst Events

**Current status:** None
**Most plausible:** Suncorp Super Netball club tokens in Australian market (favourable
regulatory environment + high crypto adoption). The Netball World Cup is the activation window.
